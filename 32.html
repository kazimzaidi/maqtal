السجاد ( عليه السلام ) وجد بني أسد مجتمعين عند القتلى  ، متحيِّرين لا يدرون ما يصنعون  ، ولم يهتدوا إلى معرفتهم  ، وقد فرَّق القوم بين رؤوسهم وأبدانهم  ، وربما يُسْألون من أهلهم وعشيرتهم ؟
So he (peace be upon him) informed them of what he had come to do — to lay these pure bodies to rest — and told them their names, just as he made known to them the Hashimites among the companions. Then weeping and wailing rose up, and tears flowed from them in every direction. The women of Asad let down their hair and struck their cheeks.
    فأخبرهم ( عليه السلام ) عمّا جاء إليه من مواراة هذه الجسوم الطاهرة  ، وأوقفهم على أسمائهم  ، كما عرَّفهم بالهاشميين من الأصحاب  ، فارتفع البكاء والعويل  ، وسالت الدموع منهم كلَّ مسيل  ، ونشرت الأسديات الشعور  ، ولطمن الخدود.
Then the Imam Zayn al-Abidin (peace be upon him) walked to his father's body, embraced it, and wept aloud. He came to the place of the grave, lifted a little of the earth, and there appeared a dug grave and a cleft tomb. He spread his palms beneath the back, saying: "In the name of God, in the path of God, upon the creed of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family). God and His Messenger have spoken truly. As God wills. There is no power and no strength save in God the Almighty." He lowered him alone and let no one of Banu Asad share in it with him, and he said to them: "With me is one who helps me." When he had settled him in his resting place, he laid his cheek upon his noble throat, saying: "Blessed is the earth that holds your pure body, for after you the world is darkened, while the Hereafter is illumined by your light. As for the night, it is sleepless, and grief is everlasting — until God chooses for your family the abode in which you now dwell. Upon you be peace from me, O son of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family), and the mercy of God and His blessings." And he wrote upon the grave: "This is the grave of al-Hussein ibn Ali ibn Abi Talib, whom they killed thirsty and a stranger."

How fine are the words of the scholar Sheikh Ali al-Jishi, may mercy be upon him, when he said:
    ولله در الحجة الشيخ علي الجشي عليه الرحمة إذ يقول :
I shall not forget when he returned from the captivity of the foes,
in secret, to bury the body of the best of the slain,
and he saw him cast down, with people gathered round about,
who had drawn back in fear of punishment.
When they saw his grief, they said: "We have indeed
come to bury the grandson of the best of messengers,
but for lifting the body and moving it we found
for all of us no power and no way."
So he called for a reed mat there and wrapped him
in it, without shroud and without washing.

The narrator said: Then he (peace be upon him) walked to his uncle al-Abbas (peace be upon him) and saw him in that state which astonished the angels among the layers of heaven, and made the houris weep in the chambers of Paradise. He fell upon him, kissing his sacred throat, saying: "After you, may the world be laid waste, O moon of Banu Hashim. Upon you from me be peace, as a martyr who sought God's reward, and the mercy of God and His blessings." He cleft a tomb for him and lowered him alone, as he had done with his martyred father, and he said to Banu Asad: "With me is one who helps me." He said: By God, is that truly so? I said: By God, it is truly so — he said it three times and I said it three times. Then he said: Rejoice, then rejoice, then rejoice. For I shall surely tell you a tale that lay with me among the hidden treasures. When there befell us at al-Taff what befell us, and my father (peace be upon him) was killed, along with those of his children, his brothers, and the rest of his family who were with him, and his sanctuary and his women were carried upon the bare backs of camels, being driven toward Kufa, I began to look at them lying slain and unburied. That weighed heavily upon my breast, and my anxiety grew intense at what I saw of them, until my soul nearly departed. My aunt Zainab the Elder, daughter of Ali, perceived this in me and said: What is it I see in you — you give up your soul, O remnant of my grandfather and my father and my brothers? I said: How should I not grieve and despair, when I see my master, my brothers, my uncles, my cousins, and my family soaked in their own blood, cast out upon the open ground, stripped, neither shrouded nor buried, with no one turning to them and no human being coming near them, as though they were a household of the Daylam and the Khazar?

She said: Let not what you see distress you. For by God, that is a covenant from the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) to your grandfather, your father, and your uncle. It is narrated that when Khadija died, the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) undertook her preparation, washing, and embalming. When he wished to shroud her, the trustworthy Gabriel descended and said: O Messenger of God, God conveys His peace to you and singles you out with greeting and honor, and says to you: O Muhammad, the shroud of Khadija — and it is from Paradise — God has gifted to her. So the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) shrouded her, first with his own noble cloak, and second with what Gabriel had brought. Thus she had two shrouds: a shroud from God, and a shroud from the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family).

And when Khadija (peace be upon her) died, the affliction upon the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) grew severe, and worries and griefs piled upon him to such a degree that he secluded himself from the people for a long while. He called that year the Year of Sorrow, because in that year he lost his uncle Abu Talib and his wife Khadija in a single year — indeed, in a single month. Then he migrated to al-Ta'if for a month, and returned to Mecca to settle there, but he could not, for the polytheists of Quraysh schemed to kill him, and gathered in the House of Assembly, and took counsel among themselves about driving him out and shedding his blood. And it is narrated that the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) also attended the funeral of Fatima bint Asad and escorted it (peace be upon her). It is said: when Fatima bint Asad, the mother of the Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him), died, Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him) came weeping, and the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family) said to him: What makes you weep? May God never make your eye weep. He said: My mother has died, O Messenger of God. The Prophet (peace be upon him and his family) said to him: Rather, my mother too, O Ali. For she used to let her own children go hungry and feed me, and leave her own children unkempt and anoint my hair. By God, in the house of Abu Talib there was a date palm, and she would race to it in the morning to gather its fruit, then pick it — may God be pleased with her — and when my cousins came out, she would give that to me. Then he (peace be upon him and his family) rose and undertook her preparation, and shrouded her in his own shirt (peace be upon him and his family). During the escorting of her funeral he would raise one foot and pause before raising the other, barefoot. When he prayed over her, he pronounced "God is greatest" seventy times, then laid her in her grave with his own noble hand, after lying down in her grave and instructing her in the testimony of faith. When the dust was poured over her and the people wished to depart, the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) began saying to her: Your son, your son, your son — not Ja'far, nor Aqil — your son, your son: Ali ibn Abi Talib.
