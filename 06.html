Who will carry to the Master of the Affair a message,
folded upon breaths each one a blaze of fire?
O son of those who, when they rise, seat death itself,
whose resolve in terror faces the edges of the swords,
The horses near you are weary of their tethers,
and the white blades grow tired within their sheaths.
The earth shall never be cleansed of the enemy's filth
as long as no flood of pouring blood runs over it.
I shield your sword from rust upon its iron
while through it these dark sorrows are not yet dispelled.
Rise! For he whose skull your blades split open
by a stroke for the faith—today is judged by it.

The Poets Coming Before the Imams (peace be upon them) and Their Weeping
Over the Imam Hussein (peace be upon him) and Their Establishing of the Mourning Assemblies
It is narrated from Abu Hamza Muhammad ibn Ya'qub, from Ja'far ibn Muhammad (peace be upon him), who said: Ali ibn al-Hussein (peace be upon him) was asked about the abundance of his weeping, and he said: Do not blame me, for Jacob (peace be upon him) lost one grandson of his children and wept until his eyes turned white from grief, though he did not know that he had died; and I looked upon fourteen men of my household slaughtered in a single morning—so do you think the grief for them will ever leave my heart?
