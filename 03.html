Sheikh al-Saduq, may God's mercy be upon him, narrated from Muhammad ibn Abd al-Rahman, from his father, from Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him), who said: While I, Fatima, Hassan, and Hussein were with the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family), he turned toward us and wept. I asked: What makes you weep, O Messenger of God? He said: I weep for what will be done to you after me. I asked: And what is that, O Messenger of God? He said: I weep for the blow you will receive upon your head, and for Fatima being struck upon her cheek, and for the stab Hassan will suffer in his thigh, and for the poison he will be made to drink, and for the killing of Hussein. He said: Then all the household wept. I asked: O Messenger of God! Has our Lord created us only for affliction? He said: Rejoice, O Ali, for God Almighty has made a covenant with me that none shall love you but a believer, and none shall hate you but a hypocrite.

Ammar ibn Abi Ammar narrated from Ibn Abbas, who said: I saw the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) as a sleeper sees, at midday, disheveled and dust-covered, in his hand a vial containing blood. It is narrated from Amir ibn Sa'd al-Bajali, who said: When Hussein ibn Ali (peace be upon him) was killed, I saw the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) in a dream, and he said: If you see al-Bara' ibn Azib, convey my greetings to him and inform him that the killers of Hussein ibn Ali are in the Fire, and that God is about to wipe out the people of the earth from him with a painful punishment. He said: So I came to al-Bara' and informed him, and he said: The Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) has spoken truly. The Messenger of God said: Whoever sees me in a dream has indeed seen me, for Satan cannot take my form.

O Hussein—by the One who sent your grandfather with guidance,
an oath of which truth shall question me—
had I been present at Karbala, I would have spent
in relieving your distress the utmost a giver can give.

The Fourth Gathering, of the First Day
The Weeping of Imam Zayn al-Abidin (peace be upon him) and His Grief for His Father, Imam Hussein (peace be upon him)
It is stated in some of the visitations of the Imams (peace be upon them): O my masters, had al-Mustafa beheld you, with the arrows of the nation sunk deep into your livers, their spears thrust into your throats, their swords drenched in your blood, the sons of harlots quenching the thirst of their wickedness against your piety and the fury of their disbelief against your faith—and you between one slain in the prayer-niche, his skull cleaved by the sword, and a martyr upon the bier whose shrouds were pierced with arrows, and one slain in the open whose head was raised upon a spear, and one shackled in prison whose limbs were crushed with iron, and one poisoned whose entrails were torn apart by gulps of poison.

The narrators have recounted that Imam Zayn al-Abidin (peace be upon him) wept for his father Hussein (peace be upon him) for twenty years, and no food was ever set before him without his weeping, until a servant of his said to him: O son of the Messenger of God, is it not time for your grief to come to an end? He said to him: Woe to you! Jacob the Prophet (peace be upon him) had twelve sons, and God caused one of them to vanish from him, and his eyes turned white from the abundance of his weeping over him, and his head turned grey from grief, and his back became bent from sorrow—while his son was still alive in this world. The Fifth Gathering, of the First Day
The Entry of Di'bil al-Khuza'i upon Imam Ali ibn Musa al-Rida (peace be upon him) during the Days of Ashura
It is narrated from Abd al-Salam ibn Salih al-Harawi, who said: Di'bil ibn Ali al-Khuza'i entered upon Ali ibn Musa al-Rida (peace be upon them both) at Marw and said to him: O son of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family), I have composed a poem about you, and I have sworn to myself that I would not recite it to anyone before you. He (peace be upon him) said: Bring it forth. So he recited to him:
المجلس الخامس  ، من اليوم الأول
دخول دعبل الخزاعي على الإمام علي بن
موسى الرضا ( عليه السلام ) في أيام عاشوراء
    روي عن عبدالسلام بن صالح الهروي قال : دخل دعبل بن علي الخزاعي على علي بن موسى الرضا ( عليهما السلام ) بمرو فقال له : يا بن رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) إني قد قلت فيك قصيدة  ، وآليت على نفسي أن لا أنشدها أحداً قبلك  ، فقال ( عليه السلام ) : هاتها فأنشده :
Schools of revealed signs, now empty of recitation,
and a dwelling of revelation, its courtyards desolate,
مدارسُ آيات خلت من تلاوة ومنزلُ وحي مُقْفِرُ العرصاتِ

I see their spoils divided among others,
while their own hands are empty of their spoils.
