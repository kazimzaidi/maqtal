Sheikh al-Mufid, may God have mercy upon him, said: Ibn Ziyad advanced toward Kufa, accompanied by Muslim ibn Amr al-Bahili, Sharik ibn al-A'war al-Harithi, his retinue, and his household, until he entered Kufa wearing a black turban with his face veiled. Word had reached the people of Hussein's (peace be upon him) approach toward them, and they were awaiting his arrival. So when they saw Ubaydullah, they supposed he was Hussein (peace be upon him). He could not pass by any group of people without their greeting him and saying: Welcome, O son of the Messenger of God! You have come at the best of times. Thus he saw in their rejoicing over Hussein (peace be upon him) what grieved him. When they grew many, Muslim ibn Amr said: Stand back—this is the governor Ubaydullah ibn Ziyad.
    وقال الشيخ المفيد عليه الرحمة : وأقبل ابن زياد إلى الكوفة  ، ومعه مسلم بن عمرو الباهلي  ، وشريك بن الأعور الحارثي  ، وحشمه وأهل بيته حتى دخل الكوفة وعليه عمامة سوداء  ، وهو متلثِّم  ، والناس قد بلغهم إقبال الحسين ( عليه السلام ) إليهم  ، فهم ينتظرون قدومه  ، فظنّوا حين رأوا عبيدالله أنه الحسين ( عليه السلام )   ، فأخذ لا يمرّ على جماعة من الناس إلاَّ سلَّموا عليه  ، وقالوا : مرحباً بك يا ابن رسول الله  ، قدمت خير مقدم  ، فرأى من تباشرهم بالحسين ( عليه السلام ) ما ساءه  ، فقال مسلم بن عمرو لمَّا أكثروا : تأخَّروا  ، هذا الأمير عبيدالله ابن زياد.
He traveled on until he reached the palace by night, and with him was a crowd that had gathered around him, having no doubt that he was Hussein (peace be upon him). Al-Nu'man ibn Bashir had locked the palace against him and his close companions. Someone with him called out for them to open the door, and al-Nu'man looked down at him, supposing him to be Hussein (peace be upon him), and said: I implore you by God, withdraw! By God, I will not surrender my trust to you, and I have no desire to fight you. He gave no reply. Then he drew near, and al-Nu'man leaned out from the height of the palace and began speaking to him, saying: Open up—may it not be opened for you—for your night has been long! A man behind him heard this and turned back to the people of Kufa who had followed him supposing he was Hussein (peace be upon him), and said: O people! It is the son of Marjana, by Him besides whom there is no god! So al-Nu'man opened for him and he entered, and they slammed the door in the faces of the people, who dispersed.
    وسار حتى وافى القصر بالليل ومعه جماعة قد التفّوا به  ، لا يشكّون أنه الحسين ( عليه السلام ) فأغلق النعمان بن بشير عليه وعلى خاصته  ، فناداه بعض من كان معه ليفتح لهم الباب  ، فاطلع عليه النعمان وهو يظنّه الحسين ( عليه السلام ) فقال : أنشدك الله إلاَّ تنحّيت  ، والله ما أنا بمسلِّم إليك أمانتي  ، ومالي في قتالك من إرب  ، فجعل لا يكلِّمه  ، ثم إنه دنا وتدلَّى النعمان من شرف القصر  ، فجعل يكلِّمه  ، فقال : افتح لا فتحت فقد طال ليلك  ، وسمعها إنسان خلفه  ، فنكص إلى القوم الذين اتّبعوه من أهل الكوفة على أنه الحسين ( عليه السلام ) فقال : يا قوم! ابن مرجانة والذي لا إله غيره  ، ففتح له النعمان فدخل  ، وضربوا الباب في وجوه الناس وانفضّوا.
When morning came, he called out among the people: Gather for the prayer! The people assembled, and he came out to them.

He praised God and extolled Him, then said: As for what follows, the Commander of the Faithful, Yazid, has appointed me over your country, your frontier, and your spoils, and has commanded me to grant justice to the wronged among you, to give to the deprived among you, and to treat well whoever listens and obeys, like a kind father—while my whip and my sword are upon whoever forsakes my command and breaks his covenant with me. So let every man fear for himself. Truth, not threat, will be the measure of you. Then he came down and took the chiefs and the people severely to task, saying: Write down for me the names of the chiefs, of those among you wanted by the Commander of the Faithful, of those among you who are Haruriyya, and of the people of doubt whose nature is opposition, hypocrisy, and discord. Whoever brings them to us is cleared, and whoever does not write down anyone for us must guarantee for us that none of those under his charge will oppose us, and that no transgressor among them will rise against us. Whoever does not do so forfeits all protection, and his blood and property become lawful to us. Any chief in whose charge there is found one of those sought by the Commander of the Faithful, whom he did not bring up to us, shall be crucified at the door of his house, and that division shall be struck from the stipend.
فحمد الله وأثنى عليه  ، ثم قال : أمّا بعد  ، فإن أمير المؤمنين يزيد ولاّني مصركم وثغركم وفيئكم  ، وأمرني بإنصاف مظلومكم وإعطاء محرومكم  ، والإحسان إلى سامعكم ومطيعكم كالوالد البرّ  ، وسوطي وسيفي على من ترك أمري وخالف عهدي  ، فليتق امرؤٌ على نفسه  ، الصدق ينبي عنك لا الوعيد  ، ثمَّ نزل  ، وأخذ العرفاء والناس أخذاً شديداً فقال : اكتبوا إليَّ العرفاء  ، ومن فيكم من طلبة أمير المؤمنين  ، ومن فيكم من أهل الحرورية  ، وأهل الريب الذين شأنهم الخلاف والنفاق والشقاق  ، فمن يجيء لنا بهم فبريء  ، ومن لم يكتب لنا أحداً فليضمن لنا من في عرافته أن لا يخالفنا منهم مخالف  ، ولا يبغي علينا باغ  ، فمن لم يفعل برئت منه الذمة وحلال لنا دمه وماله  ، أيّما عريف وجد في عرافته من بغية أمير المؤمنين أحد لم يرفعه إلينا صلب على باب داره  ، وألغيت تلك العرافة من العطاء.
When Muslim ibn Aqil (peace be upon him) heard of Ubaydullah's arrival in Kufa, of the words he had spoken, and of how he had dealt with the chiefs and the people, he left the house of al-Mukhtar and went to the house of Hani' ibn Urwa, and entered it. The Shia began to come and go to him in Hani's house, hidden and concealed from Ubaydullah, and they urged one another to secrecy.
    ولما سمع مسلم بن عقيل ( عليه السلام ) مجيء عبيدالله إلى الكوفة  ، ومقالته التي قالها  ، وما أخذ به العرفاء والناس  ، خرج من دار المختار حتى انتهى إلى دار هانىء بن عروة فدخلها  ، فأخذت الشيعة تختلف إليه في دار هانىء على تستّر واستخفاء من عبيدالله  ، وتواصوا بالكتمان.
So Ibn Ziyad summoned a freedman of his called Ma'qil and said: Take three thousand dirhams and seek out Muslim ibn Aqil, and search for his companions. When you find one of them or a group, give them these three thousand dirhams and say to them: Use this to help you in fighting your enemy. Let them know that you are one of them, for if you give them this money they will trust you and rely on you, and will hide nothing of their affairs and news from you. Then go to them morning and evening until you learn the whereabouts of Muslim ibn Aqil and gain entry to him.

So he took his pledge, exacting from him the most solemn oaths that he would be sincere and would keep the secret, and Ma'qil gave him what satisfied him. Then he said to him: Come and go to me at my house for some days, for I am seeking permission for you to see my leader. He began coming and going with the people, and sought permission for him until it was granted. Muslim ibn Aqil took his pledge and ordered Abu Thumama al-Sa'idi to take the money from him—he was the one who took charge of their funds and of what each of them gave to assist the others, with which he would buy them weapons. He was sharp-sighted and a horseman among the horsemen of the Arabs, and one of the leaders of the Shia. That man began coming and going to them, being the first to enter and the last to leave, until he understood what Ibn Ziyad needed to know of their affairs, and he would report it to him time after time.
