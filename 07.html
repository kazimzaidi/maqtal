O father of the latter martyrs, by an event
whose martyr surpassed the martyrs of old,
O you by whom, at Taff, the faith of your grandfather
was set upright, and through whom its bounds were upheld,
and you who, on the path of truth amid the edges of blades,
fell, your efforts thanked and your striving praised,
in you the proud refusers of injustice took their example, so that
its slaves could no longer rule over the free,
your guiding grandfather laid the foundations of a creed
that by your sword, on the day of Taff, you rose to raise high.
For you the faith complains of tyrants who banded
from every quarter, their armies arrayed for its war,
as though they bore a grudge against the people, and so quenched
through what they wrought their malice and their spite,
refusing that the rightful ones should rule the nation in governance,
yet content that the stranger should rule over them,
until God destroyed the party of their error,
as He had destroyed Aad and Thamud before(1)

The Second Gathering, of the Third Day
The Prophet's care for Imam Hussein (peace be upon him) and his love for him
In the noble Ziyarat al-Nahiya it is stated: By killing you they have killed Islam, and abolished prayer and fasting, and broken the traditions and the rulings, and demolished the pillars of faith, and distorted the verses of the Quran, and rushed headlong into transgression and aggression. Truly the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) has been left wronged, and the Book of God, mighty and majestic, has been forsaken, and truth was abandoned when you were overcome, conquered. With your loss were lost the takbir and the tahlil, the forbidding and the permitting, the revelation and its interpretation; and after you appeared alteration and substitution, atheism and the suspension of God's laws, vain desires and misguidance, sedition and falsehoods.

Then he said: O Yazid, have you heard the tale of the Church of the Hoof? He said: No. He said: Know that between Kaman and China there is a sea a year's journey across, with no settlement in it except a single city in the midst of the water, eighty leagues by eighty—there is none larger upon the face of the earth—and from it are carried rubies and camphor, and its trees are aloeswood and ambergris, and it is in the hands of the Christians.
