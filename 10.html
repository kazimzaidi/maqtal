The Fifth Gathering, of the Fourth Day
The killing of the two sons of Muslim ibn Aqil (peace be upon him)
Sheikh al-Saduq, may God have mercy upon him, narrated in al-Amali, saying: My father (may God have mercy upon him) related to us, saying: Ali ibn Ibrahim ibn Hashim related to us, on the authority of his father, on the authority of Ibrahim ibn Raja' al-Jahdari, on the authority of Ali ibn Jabir, who said: Uthman ibn Dawud al-Hashimi related to me, on the authority of Muhammad ibn Muslim, on the authority of Humran ibn A'yan, on the authority of Abu Muhammad, an elder of the people of Kufa, who said: When Hussein ibn Ali (peace be upon them) was killed, two young boys were taken captive from his camp and brought to Ubaydullah ibn Ziyad. He summoned a jailer of his and said: Take these two boys to yourself. Feed them nothing of good food, give them nothing cool to drink, and keep their imprisonment narrow upon them. The two boys would fast by day, and when night fell upon them, they were brought two barley loaves and a jug of fresh water.
