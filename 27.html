The scholars of hadith narrated: that al-Hussein (peace be upon him) made his bequest to his son Ali ibn al-Hussein (peace be upon them), and delivered to him the Greatest Name, the inheritances of the prophets, and explicitly designated him for the Imamate after him. In another tradition: that al-Hussein (peace be upon him), at the time of his battle at Karbala, summoned Ali ibn al-Hussein (peace be upon them), who was ill, and made his bequest to him concerning the Greatest Name and the inheritances of the prophets (peace be upon them), and informed him that he had handed the sciences, the scriptures, and the weapons to Umm Salama, and had ordered her to deliver all of that to him (3).

Then he went to the tents of the women, and came to the tent of his son Zayn al-Abidin (peace be upon him), and found him cast down upon a leather mat. He entered upon him, with Zainab nursing him. When Ali ibn al-Hussein (peace be upon them) looked at him, he wanted to rise but could not due to the severity of his illness. So he said to his aunt: Support me against your chest, for this is the son of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) who has come. So Zainab sat behind him and supported him against her chest. Al-Hussein (peace be upon him) began to ask his son about his illness, while he was praising God Almighty. Then he said: O father! When he asked about his uncle, Zainab choked back her tears, and began watching her brother to see how he would answer him, because he had not told him of the martyrdom of his uncle al-Abbas, out of fear that his illness would worsen.

He (peace be upon him) said: O my son, your uncle has been killed, and they severed his two hands on the bank of the Euphrates. Ali ibn al-Hussein (peace be upon him) wept bitterly until he fainted. When he recovered from his faint, he began asking about each of his uncles, and al-Hussein (peace be upon him) would say to him: He has been killed. He said: And where is my brother Ali, and Habib ibn Mudhahir, and Muslim ibn Awsaja, and Zuhayr ibn al-Qayn? He said to him: O my son, know that there is no living man in the tents except you and me, and as for those you ask about, they all lie fallen upon the face of the earth.
