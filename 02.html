How excellent are the words of Sheikh Abdul-Husayn Shukr, may mercy be upon him, when he says in lamenting Imam Hassan (peace be upon him):
    ولله درّ الشيخ عبدالحسين شكر عليه الرحمة إذ يقول في رثاء الإمام الحسن ( عليه السلام ) :
Who will convey to the Chosen One and to the pure Fatima
that Hussein weeps tears of blood over Hassan?
He calls to him: O my support in every calamity,
my helper when time strikes me with weakness.
You were to me, of the sons of glory, their last remnant,
and against the enemy my spear that did not soften through you.
But today, after you, it has grown soft
for anyone who pokes at it, and life's ease is no longer sweet.
My grief for Zainab, who calls to him while her eye
weeps and her tears pour like clouds streaming with rain.(1)
من مبلغُ المصطفى والطهرِ فاطمة أن الحسينَ دماً يبكي على الحَسَنِ يدعوه يا عضدي في كلِّ نائبة ومُسْعِدي إن رماني الدهرُ بالوَهَنِ قد كنتَ لي من بني العليا بقيَّتَهم وللعدوِّ قناتي فيك لم تَلِنِ فاليومَ بعدَك أضحت وهي ليِّنةٌ لغامز وهنيُّ العيشِ غيرُ هني لهفي لزينبَ تدعوه ومقلتُها عبرى وأدمعُها كالعارضِ الهَتِنِ (1)
The completion of the hadith: He (peace be upon him and his family) said: As for Hussein, he is of me, and he is my son and my child, and the best of creation after his brother. He is the Imam of the Muslims, the master of the believers, the vicegerent of the Lord of the worlds, the refuge of those who seek help, the shelter of those who seek protection, and the proof of God over all His creation. He is the master of the youth of Paradise, and the gate of salvation for the nation. His command is my command, and obedience to him is obedience to me. Whoever follows him is of me, and whoever disobeys him is not of me. Indeed, when I saw him I remembered what would be done to him after me. It is as if I see him having sought refuge at my sanctuary and my grave, yet finding no protection. So I draw him to my breast in his sleep, and command him to depart from the land of my migration, and give him glad tidings of martyrdom. So he departs from it to the land of his slaying and the place of his fall — a land of grief and affliction, of killing and destruction. A band of Muslims supports him; they are among the chiefs of the martyrs of my nation on the Day of Resurrection. It is as if I behold him struck by an arrow, falling slain from his horse, then slaughtered as a ram is slaughtered, wronged.

How excellent are the words of the scholar Sheikh Hasan Ali al-Badr, may mercy be upon him, when he says:
    ولله درّ الحجة الشيخ حسن علي البدر عليه الرحمة إذ يقول :
Have you not seen how the people of God were heaped
with the calamities of fate, in what dreadful heaping?
Did not the daughter of the Prophet choke on her own saliva,
as the enemies made her swallow the taste of bitter colocynth?
Was not al-Karrar slain in transgression by the sword of one
who rebelled and tyrannized in the sins he committed —
the enemy of the God of the worlds, the son of Muljam,
the most wretched of all people since the days of Adam?
Did not the pure one's son return, forced
into a peace with war though he was no peacemaker?
Did they not storm his tent and plunder
his baggage there, as raiders plunder spoils?
Did the enemies not treacherously slip him poison,
so he cast into the basin the heart of all noble virtues?
Did they not shoot him with arrows while he was a corpse
upon the bier — nay, above the heads of the stars?
And if I forget, I shall never forget Hussein when he became,
in spite of the religion's nose, plunder for the swords.
He perished after the vastness of space grew narrow for him,
so the expanse of the worlds grew narrow with grief for him.
He perished, his heart burning with thirst,
amid sorrows in which every Hashimite perished.
Why then does Nizar not rise to avenge them,
and suckle war from the udders of sharp blades,
and fill it with horses racing in their advance
against the family of Harb beneath fierce lions,
so those hooves trample their heads
as they trampled the breast of the master of Hashim?(1)

Al-Rawandi, may mercy be upon him, narrated that the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family) was one day sitting with Ali, Fatima, Hassan, and Hussein (peace be upon them) around him. He said to them: How will it be with you when you are slain and your graves are scattered apart? Hassan (peace be upon him) said: Will we die a natural death or be killed? He said: O my son, rather you will be killed by poison, wronged; your brother will be killed by thirst; your father will be killed unjustly; and your descendants will be driven and scattered across the earth.

It is related in the commentary of Imam al-Askari (peace be upon him) that he said regarding the words of the Almighty: "And when We took your covenant — up to His words — so the punishment will not be lightened for them, nor will they be helped"(2). The Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) — when this verse was revealed concerning the Jews, those Jews who broke the covenant of God, denied the messengers of God, and killed the friends of God — said: Shall I not inform you of who among the Jews of this nation resembles them? They said: Yes, O Messenger of God. He said: A people of my nation who claim to be of my religion, who kill the most virtuous of my descendants and the purest of my lineage, who alter my law and my Sunnah, and who kill my two sons Hassan and Hussein as the predecessors of these Jews killed Zakariyya and Yahya. Indeed, God curses them as He cursed them, and He will send against the remnant of their descendants, before the Day of Resurrection, a guide rightly guided from the descendants of the wronged Hussein, who will drive them with the swords of his friends into the fire of Hell.(3)

    فقلت : بأبي أنت وأمي  ، فعلت في هذا اليوم وفي اليوم الأول فما هو ؟ قال : أبكي على ابني هذا  ، تقتله فئة باغية كافرة من بني أمية لعنهم الله  ، لا أنالهم الله شفاعتي يوم القيامة  ، يقتله رجل يثلم الدين ويكفر بالله العظيم  ، ثمَّ قال : اللهم إني أسألك فيهما ما سألك إبراهيم في ذريّته  ، اللهم أحبَّهما وأحبَّ من يحبُّهما  ، والعن من يبغضهما ملء السماء والأرض (1) ولله درّ الحجّة العظيم الشيخ محمد جواد البلاغي عليه الرحمة إذ يقول :
The heart of the Chosen One was gladdened with joy by the grandson,
had not the one announcing the news of al-Tuff alarmed him.
He saw him the best of children, by whom refuge is sought,
and the best of martyrs in the faith, defending it.
Though Fatima rejoiced on the day of his birth,
on the night of al-Tuff she became one who weeps for him.
Or though her heart revived from the light of his countenance,
the flow of crimson tears was given in turn over him.

Ibn Qulawayh, may mercy be upon him, narrated from Abu Ja'far (peace be upon him), who said: When Hussein (peace be upon him) entered, the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) would draw him to himself, then say to the Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him): Hold him. Then he would bend over him, kiss him, and weep. So he would say: O father, why do you weep? He would say: O my son, I kiss the place of the swords on you and weep. He said: O father, will I be killed? He said: Yes, by God, and your father and your brother and you. He said: O father, will our resting places be scattered apart? He said: Yes, O my son. He said: Who among your nation will visit us? He said: None will visit me and visit your father and your brother and you except the truthful ones of my nation.

    روى ابن قولويه عليه الرحمة  ، عن أبي جعفر ( عليه السلام ) قال : كان رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) إذا دخل الحسين ( عليه السلام ) اجتذبه إليه  ، ثمَّ يقول لأمير المؤمنين ( عليه السلام ) : أمسكه  ، ثم يقع عليه فيقبِّله ويبكي  ، فيقول : يا أبه  ، لم تبكي ؟ فيقول : يا بنيَّ  ، أقبِّل موضع السيوف منك وأبكي  ، قال : يا أبه  ، وأقتل ؟ قال : إي والله وأبوك وأخوك وأنت  ، قال : يا أبه  ، فمصارعُنا شتى ؟ قال : نعم يا بني  ، قال : فمن يزورنا من أمتك ؟ قال : لا يزورني ويزور أباك وأخاك وأنت إلاَّ الصدّيقون من أمتي (2) .
Ibn Nama, may mercy be upon him, narrated in Mathir al-Ahzan, from Ibn Abbas, who said: When the illness from which he died grew severe upon the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family), he drew Hussein (peace be upon him) to his breast, his sweat flowing upon him as he gave up his soul, and he was saying: What is between me and Yazid — may God not bless him! O God, curse Yazid. Then he fainted for a long while and revived, and began to kiss Hussein while his eyes overflowed, saying: Indeed, I and your killer have a standing-place before God Almighty.
