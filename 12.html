Al-Hurr said to him: By God, I do not know anything of these letters and messengers you mention. Hussein (peace be upon him) said to him: Death is nearer to you than that. Then he said to his companions: Rise and mount. So they mounted, and he waited until his womenfolk had mounted. Then he said to his companions: Turn back. But when they went to turn back, the people came between them and their departure. So Hussein (peace be upon him) said to al-Hurr: May your mother be bereaved of you, what do you want? Hussein (peace be upon him) said to him: Then what do you want? He said: I want to take you to the governor Ubaydullah ibn Ziyad. He said: Then by God, I will not follow you. He said: Then by God, I will not let you go. They went back and forth in this exchange three times. When the words between them grew many, al-Hurr said to him: I was not commanded to fight you. I was only commanded not to part from you until I bring you to Kufa. So if you refuse, take a road that neither leads you into Kufa nor returns you to Medina, that I may have a way out between you and me, until I write to the governor Ubaydullah ibn Ziyad. Perhaps God may grant me deliverance from being tested with anything concerning your affair. So take this way here.

And how excellent are the words of Ibn al-Arandas, may God's mercy be upon him, when he says:
    ولله درّ ابن العرندس عليه الرحمة إذ يقول :
O dwellers of the land of al-Tuff, upon you be
the greeting of a lover who has no patience away from you.
I have unfurled the volumes of praise after folding them,
and on every page of my eulogy for you is a line.
My verse for you matches the tears of my eye:
this whitened is poetry, and this reddened is prose.
Do not accuse me of having found solace, for indeed
the appointments of my consolation—by your right—are the Day of Gathering.
My lowliness through you is honor, and my poverty through you is wealth,
my hardship through you is ease, and my brokenness through you is mended.
The lightning of the clouds glistens for me from your abodes,
and from my tears a downpour pours for its flashing.
So my eyes, like al-Khansa, shed their tears,
while my heart, in its love for you, is hard as rock.
I stood at the abode where you once dwelt,
but your dwelling, after your departure, is a wasteland.
Its lessons have been effaced, though long
there were taught the knowledge of the Divine and the remembrance.
Clouds of my tears poured down upon it
until the moringa and lote-tree were watered with weeping.
The parting after your distance was for me like the soul's departure,
and the thought circled my mind at the trace of the abode.
The clouds have withdrawn from it and brought no rain,
nor has any rain fallen for it after Hussein.
The Imam of guidance, the grandson of prophethood, the father of
the Imams, the master of intellect, the lord to whom belongs command.(2)
