In Ma'ali al-Sibtayn by al-Ha'iri, may God have mercy on him, in the biography of Ali al-Akbar (peace be upon him), he says: His mother was Layla, daughter of Abu Murra ibn Urwa ibn Mas'ud al-Thaqafi. Urwa was one of the four chieftains in Islam, and Ali ibn al-Husayn (peace be upon him) inherited nobility and leadership from both sides. It is narrated in Nafas al-Mahmum: The Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) said: There are four chieftains in Islam — Bishr ibn Hilal al-Abdi, Adi ibn Hatim, Suraqa ibn Malik al-Mudliji, and Urwa ibn Mas'ud al-Thaqafi. Urwa was one of the two great men referred to in the words of God Almighty, quoting the disbelievers of Quraysh: "And they said: Why was this Quran not sent down upon a great man from the two towns?"(3)

As for Layla, the mother of Ali al-Akbar, her mother was Maymuna, daughter of Abu Sufyan ibn Harb ibn Umayya, known as Umm Shayba. Mu'awiya was thus the maternal uncle of Layla, the mother of Ali al-Akbar. For this reason, a man from the people of Kufa called out to Ali al-Akbar when he came forth for battle: You have kinship with the Commander of the Faithful Yazid; if you wish, we will grant you safety. He replied: Woe to you! The kinship of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) is more deserving to be honored. Mu'awiya often praised Ali ibn al-Husayn (peace be upon him), to the point that he once said to his companions: Who is most deserving of the caliphate among the people? They said: You. He said: No — rather, the most deserving of the caliphate is Ali ibn al-Husayn ibn Ali (peace be upon them); his grandfather is the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family). In him is the courage of the Banu Hashim, the generosity of the Banu Umayya,(1) and the splendor of Thaqif — meaning fine appearance.
    وليلى أم علي الأكبر أمُّها ميمونة بنت أبي سفيان بن حرب بن أمية  ، المكنَّاة بأم شيبة  ، وكان معاوية خال ليلى أم علي الأكبر  ، ولهذا ناداه رجل من أهل الكوفة حين برز علي الأكبر للقتال : إن لك رحماً بأمير المؤمنين يزيد  ، فإن شئت آمنّاك  ، فقال له : ويلك  ، لقرابة رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) أحقّ أن تُرعى  ، وكان معاوية كثيراً يمدح علي بن الحسين ( عليه السلام ) حتى قال يوماً لأصحابه : من أحق الناس بالخلافة ؟ قالوا : أنت  ، قال : لا  ، بل أحق الناس بالخلافة علي بن الحسين بن علي ( عليهم السلام ) جدّه رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله )   ، وفيه شجاعة بني هاشم  ، وسخاء بني أمية (1)   ، وزهو ثقيف  ، يعني المنظر الحسن.
It is narrated from Abu Ubayda and Khalaf al-Ahmar that these verses were composed about Ali ibn al-Husayn the Elder, the one slain at Karbala:
    روي عن أبي عبيدة  ، وخلف الأحمر  ، أن هذه الأبيات قيلت في علي بن الحسين الأكبر  ، المقتول بكربلاء :
No eye that gazed has ever seen his like,
whether barefoot walking or in sandals shod.
He would boil the raw meat until, when
it was cooked, it cost the eater nothing.
When his fire was kindled for him,
he lit it upon the lofty height ahead
so that the destitute and impoverished might see it,
or the lone traveler who has no settled home.
I mean the son of Layla, the man of bounty and giving,
I mean the son of the daughter of noble lineage.
He does not prefer this world over his religion,
nor does he sell the truth for falsehood.

It was the custom among the Arabs who loved guests and went to great lengths to honor them to light a fire atop their houses in summer and winter, on dark nights, so that if a guest came to them from afar in the darkness of night, that fire would guide him to the host's home — that he might not stray or lose his way. They called it the fire of hospitality. Out of his great love for guests and his eagerness to honor them, Ali al-Akbar (peace be upon him), when he lit the fire atop his house, would light it large and ablaze, so that the destitute, the poor, the impoverished, and the orphan might see it and come down to his home and to his food. And how could it be otherwise, when he was the master of generosity, bounty, virtue, and giving, nursed in honor and noble lineage? And he (peace be upon him) held such firm standing in faith and certainty that he would not prefer his worldly life over his religion, nor sell the truth for falsehood.

Ali al-Akbar (peace be upon him) was a youth of handsome features and beautiful appearance, without equal, renowned in courage, and likewise in the qualities of perfection — majesty, greatness, generosity, fine character, and more.(1)

Ali al-Akbar (peace be upon him) was the most beautiful of people in countenance, the finest of them in form, character, and speech, and the most resembling of people to the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) — and this by the testimony of the Master of the Youths of Paradise (peace be upon him). For it is narrated that when he sought his father's permission to fight, his father looked at him with a despairing glance, then lowered his eyes and wept. He then raised his two index fingers toward the heavens and said: O God, be You the witness against them, for there has gone forth against them a youth who most resembles Your Messenger in form, character, and speech. Whenever we longed for Your Prophet, we would gaze upon him.(2)

The proof of God, Shaykh Ali al-Jishi, may God have mercy on him, says, speaking on behalf of al-Husayn (peace be upon him):
    ويقول الحجّة الشيخ علي الجشي عليه الرحمة على لسان الحسين ( عليه السلام ) :
O likeness of my grandfather in speech and noble traits,

The Second Gathering of the Ninth Day
The Martyrdom of Ali al-Akbar (peace be upon him)
For the pure ones of the household of Muhammad and Ali, may God's blessings be upon them both and upon their families, let the weepers weep; for them let the mourners lament; for the likes of them let tears flow, let those who cry out cry out, those who clamor clamor, and those who wail wail. Where is al-Hassan, and where is al-Husayn? Where are the sons of al-Husayn — righteous one after righteous one, truthful one after truthful one? Where is the path after the path, the chosen after the chosen? Where are the rising suns? Where are the shining moons? Where are the radiant stars? Where are the beacons of religion and the foundations of knowledge?(3)

It is stated in Maqatil al-Talibiyyin that Ali ibn al-Husayn (peace be upon him) was born during the caliphate of Uthman, and he narrated from his grandfather Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him).(1)
    جاء في مقاتل الطالبيين أن علي بن الحسين ( عليه السلام ) ولد في خلافة عثمان وقد روى عن جدّه علي بن أبي طالب ( عليه السلام ) (1) .
In Ma'ali al-Sibtayn he says: When the people of Medina longed for the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family), they would look at Ali al-Akbar (peace be upon him). Al-Husayn (peace be upon him) loved him so intensely that whenever he saw him he rejoiced and was overjoyed with great delight, and whenever he asked him for something he would never refuse him, even if it required a miracle. Kathir ibn Shadhan said: I witnessed al-Husayn ibn Ali (peace be upon them) when his son Ali al-Akbar craved grapes from him out of season, so he struck his hand against a pillar of the mosque and brought forth for him grapes and bananas and fed him, and he (peace be upon him) said: What God has for His friends is more abundant still.(2)
    وفي معالي السبطين قال : وكان أهل المدينة إذا اشتاقوا إلى النبي ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) نظروا إلى علي الأكبر ( عليه السلام )   ، وكان الحسين ( عليه السلام ) يحبه حباً شديداً بحيث إذا رآه فرح به وسرَّ سروراً عظيماً  ، وإذا سأله حاجة لا يردّه أبداً ولو على سبيل الإعجاز  ، قال كثير ابن شاذان : شهدت الحسين بن علي ( عليهما السلام ) وقد اشتهى عليه ابنه علي الأكبر عنباً في غير أوانه  ، فضرب بيده إلى سارية المسجد  ، فأخرج له عنباً وموزاً فأطعمه  ، وقال ( عليه السلام ) : ما عند الله لأوليائه أكثر (2) .

From Abdullah ibn Bukayr in a long hadith, who said: I made the pilgrimage with Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him) and said: O son of the Messenger of God, if the grave of al-Husayn ibn Ali (peace be upon them) were dug up, would anything be found of him in his grave? He said: O son of Bukayr, how grave is your question! Indeed al-Husayn ibn Ali (peace be upon him) is with his father, his mother, and his brother in the dwelling of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family), and with him — they are provided for and made joyful.
