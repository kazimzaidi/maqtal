When the dust cleared, there was Hussein (peace be upon him) standing over the youth, who was thrashing his legs. Hussein (peace be upon him) said: "By God, it is hard upon your uncle that you should call him and he not answer you, or that he answer you but cannot help you, or help you but it avails you nothing.     فانجلت الغبرة فإذا بالحسين ( عليه السلام ) قائم على رأس الغلام  ، وهو يفحص برجله  ، فقال الحسين ( عليه السلام ) : يعزّ والله على عمك أن تدعوه فلا يجيبك  ، أو يجيبك فلا يعينك  ، أو يعينك فلا يغني عنك  ، بعداً لقوم قتلوك  ، ثمَّ احتمله  ، فكأني أنظر إلى رجلي الغلام يخطّان في الأرض  ، وقد وضع صدره على صدره  ، فقلت في نفسي : ما يصنع ؟ فجاء حتى ألقاه بين القتلى من أهل بيته.
Then he said: "O God, count them in number, kill them scattered, leave not one of them, and never forgive them. Patience, O sons of my uncles! Patience, O my household! May you never see humiliation after this day!" (1).
    ثم قال : اللهم أحصهم عدداً  ، واقتلهم بدداً  ، ولا تغادر منهم أحداً  ، ولا تغفر لهم أبداً  ، صبراً يا بني عمومتي  ، صبراً يا أهل بيتي  ، ولا رأيتم هواناً بعد هذا اليوم أبداً (1) .
In another narration, also from Humayd ibn Muslim, he said: Hussein (peace be upon him) said: "Away with a people who killed you—their adversary concerning you on the Day of Resurrection will be the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family)." Then he said: "It is hard upon your uncle that you should call him and he not answer you, or that he answer you yet his answer avail you nothing, on a day when his slain are many and his helpers few." Then he carried him upon his chest—and it is as though I am still gazing at the youth's legs trailing along the ground—until he laid him beside his own son, Ali ibn al-Hussein (peace be upon him). I asked about the youth, and they said: He is al-Qasim ibn al-Hasan ibn Ali ibn Abi Talib, may the blessings of God be upon them all (2).
    وفي رواية عن حميد بن مسلم أيضاً قال : قال الحسين ( عليه السلام ) : بعداً لقوم قتلوك  ، خصمهم فيك يوم القيامة رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله )   ، ثمَّ قال : عزَّ على عمك أن تدعوه فلا يجيبك  ، أو يجيبك ثم لا تنفعك إجابته  ، يوم كثر واتره  ، وقلَّ ناصره  ، ثمَّ احتمله على صدره  ، وكأني أنظر إلى رجلي الغلام تخطّان في الأرض حتى ألقاه مع ابنه علي ابن الحسين ( عليه السلام )   ، فسألت عن الغلام فقالوا : هو القاسم بن الحسن بن علي بن أبي طالب صلوات الله عليهم أجمعين (2) .
It is narrated from Imam al-Sadiq (peace be upon him) that he said: Hussein ibn Ali (peace be upon them both) used to lay his slain one upon another, then say: "Our slain are the slain of the prophets and the family of the prophets" (3).
    وروي عن الإمام الصادق ( عليه السلام ) أنه قال : كان الحسين بن علي ( عليهما السلام ) يضع قتلاه بعضهم على بعض  ، ثم يقول : قتلانا قتلى النبيين وآل النبيين (3) .

Full moons from the forelock of Hashim,
foremost in virtue, their racecourse the heights of glory.
Like the full moon ringed by a halo of stars,
like the sun encircled by its moons.
They are like lions—nay, the lions stand in awe of them;
the lions dread them, so where is their lineage beside his?
Their deeds were pure, so their fragrance grew sweet,
and the horizons were perfumed by their scent.
They took, on the Day of Taff, the noblest of stands,
wherein the eyes of glory found their settled rest.
They made clear the paths of the sacred Law, so it became
a shining beacon to those who travel its way.
They traced for the people of truth the road of refusal,
and by their deeds its ramparts were built.
They demolished by force the fortresses of tyranny when
they rose up, and its despot was left abased.
They reminded their enemies of Badr and of what
their blades had wrought upon their forefathers there.
They blocked the open ground of Taff with their corpses,
while the horses made their pasture over the bodies.
They did not quit the battle until they were felled
upon the dust, their pillows its stones.
My soul be the ransom for the grandson of Ahmad, alone,
while around him in battle the wicked closed in.
I ransom him, alone, finding no helper, when
the wastelands were sealed against him by the foe (1).

Sheikh al-Mufid, may God's mercy be upon him, said in al-Irshad, regarding the number of the children of Imam al-Hasan al-Mujtaba (peace be upon him), that they were fifteen, male and female: Zayd ibn al-Hasan, and his two sisters Umm al-Hasan and Umm al-Hussein—their mother was Umm Bashir bint Abi Mas'ud ibn Uqba ibn Amr ibn Tha'laba al-Khazrajiyya; al-Hasan ibn al-Hasan, whose mother was Khawla bint Manzur al-Fazariyya; Amr ibn al-Hasan and his two brothers al-Qasim and Abdullah, the sons of al-Hasan, whose mother was a freed slave-woman (umm walad); Abd al-Rahman ibn al-Hasan, whose mother was a freed slave-woman; al-Hussein ibn al-Hasan, surnamed al-Athram, and his brother Talha ibn al-Hasan, and their sister Fatima bint al-Hasan—their mother was Umm Ishaq bint Talha ibn Ubaydullah al-Taymi; and Umm Abdullah, Fatima the younger, Umm Salama, and Ruqayya, the daughters of al-Hasan, by various mothers (1).
    قال الشيخ المفيد عليه الرحمة في الإرشاد في عدد أولاد الإمام الحسن المجتبى ( عليه السلام ) أنّهم : خمسة عشر ذكراً وأنثى : زيد بن الحسن  ، وأختاه أم الحسن وأم الحسين  ، أمهم أم بشير بنت أبي مسعود بن عقبة بن عمرو بن ثعلبة الخزرجيّة  ، والحسن بن الحسن  ، أمّه خولة بنت منظور الفزارية  ، وعمرو بن الحسن وأخواه القاسم وعبدالله ابنا الحسن  ، أمهم أم ولد  ، وعبدالرحمن بن الحسن  ، أمُّه أم ولد  ، والحسين بن الحسن الملقَّب بالأثرم  ، وأخوة طلحة بن الحسن  ، وأختهما فاطمة بنت الحسن  ، أمهم أم إسحاق بنت طلحة بن عبيدالله التيمي  ، وأم عبدالله  ، وفاطمة الصغرى  ، وأم سلمة  ، ورقية بنات الحسن  ، لأمهات شتى (1) .
As for Abd al-Rahman, he went out with his uncle Hussein (peace be upon him) on the pilgrimage and died at al-Abwa' while in the state of ihram. As for al-Hussein ibn al-Hasan, known as al-Athram, he possessed virtue and devotion, and left no progeny. Talha ibn al-Hasan was generous, abundant in giving and charity. As for Amr ibn al-Hasan, he was with Hussein at Karbala and was martyred.
    فأما عبد الرحمن فإنه خرج مع عمّه الحسين ( عليه السلام ) إلى الحجّ  ، وتوفيِّ بالأبواء وهو محرم  ، وأما الحسين بن الحسن المعروف بالأثرم كان له فضل وعبادة  ، ولا بقية له  ، وطلحة بن الحسن كان جواداً كثير العطاء والصدقات  ، وأما عمرو بن الحسن فكان مع الحسين بكربلاء واستشهد.
As for al-Hasan ibn al-Hasan (peace be upon him), he was with his uncle Hussein (peace be upon him) at Karbala, and fought beside him with the struggle of a valiant lion, and bore with him the descending calamity to the utmost, until he was riddled with wounds and lay motionless, unable to stir, until his uncle Hussein was killed. When the enemies of God came to finish off the wounded, they found al-Hasan ibn al-Hasan lying among the slain with breath still in him, and they wished to finish him off. But Asma' ibn Kharija recognized him—for there was kinship between him and Khawla—and prevented them, saying: "By God, I will never let you finish off the son of Khawla!"
    وأمّا الحسن بن الحسن ( عليه السلام ) فإنه كان مع عمّه الحسين ( عليه السلام ) بكربلاء  ، فجاهد معه جهاد الأسد الباسل  ، وبالغ معه على احتمال الخطب النازل  ، حتى أُثخن بالجراح  ، وبقي ملقىً لم يكن به حراك  ، إلى أن قُتل عمُّه الحسين  ، وأتى أعداء الله للتجهيز على الجرحى  ، فوجدوا الحسن بن الحسن ملقىً بين القتلى وبه نفس  ، فأرادوا أن يجهزوا عليه  ، فعرفه أسماء بن خارجة وكان بينه وبين خولة قرابة  ، فمنعهم عنه  ، وقال : والله لا أدعكم تُجهزون على ابن خولة أبداً.
The mother of al-Hasan ibn al-Hasan (peace be upon him) was Khawla al-Fazariyya, whose mother was Mulayka, the sister of Asma' ibn Kharija. So Umar ibn Sa'd said: "Leave him to Abu Hassan, his sister's son." So he was left, and Asma' ibn Kharija took him and carried him to his home, where he stayed treating his wounds until he recovered and returned to Medina.
    وكانت أم الحسن بن الحسن ( عليه السلام ) خولة الفزارية  ، أمّها مليكة أخت أسماء بن خارجة  ، فقال عمر بن سعد : اتركوه لأبي حسّان ابن أخته  ، فترك  ، فأخذه أسماء بن خارجة وحمله إلى منزله  ، فبقي يعالج جراحاته حتى برىء ورجع إلى المدينة.

Al-Hasan ibn al-Hasan (peace be upon him) was a man of high standing, virtuous, pious, and learned. He administered the charitable endowments of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) by the leave of Ali ibn al-Hussein Zayn al-Abidin (peace be upon him). He has a story with al-Hajjaj, narrated by al-Zubayr ibn Bakkar, who said: Al-Hasan ibn al-Hasan was the administrator of the charitable endowments of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) in his time.
    وكان الحسن بن الحسن ( عليه السلام ) جليلا  ، فاضلا  ، ورعاً  ، عالماً  ، وكان يلي صدقات رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) بإجازة علي بن الحسين زين العابدين ( عليه السلام ) وله مع الحجّاج خبر رواه الزبير بن بكار  ، قال : وكان الحسن بن الحسن والياً على صدقات رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) في عصره.
It is narrated that al-Hasan ibn al-Hasan asked his uncle Hussein (peace be upon him) for the hand of one of his two daughters. Hussein (peace be upon him) said to him: "Choose whichever you wish." Al-Hasan grew shy and gave no answer. So Hussein said to him: "My son, I have chosen for you my daughter Fatima, for she most resembles my mother Fatima bint Muhammad." And he married him to her. Al-Hasan ibn al-Hasan died at the age of thirty-five. When he died, may God have mercy on him, Fatima bint al-Hussein (peace be upon him) pitched a tent and recited the Qur'an by his grave, fasting by day and standing in prayer by night.
    وروي أن الحسن بن الحسن خطب إلى عمّه الحسين ( عليه السلام ) إحدى ابنتيه  ، فقال له الحسين ( عليه السلام ) : اختر أيهما شئت  ، فاستحى الحسن ولم يردَّ جواباً  ، فقال له الحسين : يا بنيّ  ، إني اخترت لك ابنتي فاطمة  ، فهي أكثر شبهاً بأمي فاطمة بنت محمد  ، فزوَّجه بها  ، وقبض الحسن بن الحسن وله خمس وثلاثون سنة  ، ولما مات رحمه الله ضربت فاطمة بنت الحسين ( عليه السلام ) فسطاطاً تقرأ عنده القرآن  ، وكانت تصوم النهار وتقوم الليل.
As for Zayd ibn al-Hasan, he was with his uncle Hussein at Karbala. He was young, not yet of age, and did not fight. He was taken captive among the prisoners and brought along with Ali ibn al-Hussein (peace be upon them) and the rest of the women and children to Syria, where they were brought before Yazid in the most wretched state and condition.
    وأما زيد بن الحسن  ، فإنه كان مع عمه الحسين بكربلاء  ، وكان صغيراً لم يراهق ولم يقاتل  ، وأخذ أسيراً مع الأسارى  ، وسير به مع علي بن الحسين ( عليهما السلام ) وباقي الحرم والأطفال إلى الشام  ، وأدخلوا على يزيد في أسوأ حال ومقام.
It is narrated that one day he was sitting before Yazid ibn Mu'awiya, whose son Khalid was sitting with him. Yazid said to Zayd ibn al-Hasan (peace be upon him): "Will you wrestle my son Khalid?" He replied: "No, but give him a knife and give me a knife, and I will fight him." Yazid ibn Mu'awiya said: "A trait I know well from Akhzam—does the serpent beget anything but a serpent?" He meant that the boy intended to kill his son in his presence.
    روي أنه كان ذات يوم جالساً بين يدي يزيد بن معاوية  ، وكان ولده خالد جالساً معه  ، فقال يزيد لزيد بن الحسن ( عليه السلام ) : أتصارع ابني خالداً ؟ فقال : لا  ، ولكن أعطه سكيناً وأعطني سكيناً وأقاتله  ، فقال يزيد بن معاوية : شنشنة أعرفها من أخزم  ، هل تلد الحيّة إلاَّ حية ؟ يريد أن يقتل ابني بمحضري.
Then Zayd returned to Medina with Ali ibn al-Hussein and the womenfolk of Hussein and settled there. Zayd was of high rank, generous of nature, refined of soul, abundant in good works. He administered the charitable endowments of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family). The poets praised him, and people sought him out from distant lands to seek his bounty. Zayd lived to the age of ninety (1).
    ثم إن زيد رجع إلى المدينة مع علي بن الحسين وحرم الحسين وأقام بها  ، وكان زيد جليل القدر  ، كريم الطبع  ، ظريف النفس  ، كثير البرّ  ، وكان يتولَّى صدقات رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله )   ، ومدحه الشعراء  ، وقصده الناس من الآفاق لطلب فضله  ، وأسنّ زيد حتى بلغ تسعين سنة (1)

Abdullah ibn al-Hasan was with his uncle Hussein (peace be upon him). He was young, not yet of age, eleven years old. When the supporters of Hussein (peace be upon him) had perished and he resolved to meet the enemy himself, he came to bid farewell to his women. Abdullah heard his uncle Hussein's farewell and his last counsel, and when Hussein left the tent, Abdullah followed him. Hussein called out to the women: "Hold him back!" So the women came out to bring him back.
    وعبدالله بن الحسن كان مع عمّه الحسين ( عليه السلام )   ، وكان صغيراً لم يراهق  ، ابن إحدى عشرة سنة  ، فلمَّا فني أنصار الحسين ( عليه السلام ) وعزم على لقاء الأعداء بنفسه أتى مودِّعاً لنسائه  ، فسمع عبدالله وداع عمّه الحسين ( عليه السلام ) والوصيَّة به  ، فلمَّا خرج الحسين من الخيمة لحقه عبدالله فصاح الحسين بالنساء  ، أمسكنه  ، فخرجن النساء ليرددنه.
He said: "Leave me! By God, I will not part from my uncle, or I will die before him." He broke free from the women's hands and caught up with his uncle. Abdullah saw Murra ibn Munqidh al-Azdi bearing down upon Hussein (peace be upon him) with his sword, and he cried out: "O son of the adulteress, would you kill my uncle?" Murra turned to him and struck him with his sword. The boy shielded himself with his hand, and it was severed at the forearm. Abdullah cried: "O uncle, help me!" So Hussein swooped down upon him as the falcon swoops upon its prey. He came to him and killed Murra. Al-Qunduzi al-Hanafi narrated from the Maqtal of Abu Mikhnaf that when Imam Zayn al-Abidin (peace be upon him) reached Medina from captivity, he addressed the people of Medina and said in his sermon: "O people, we have risen this morning exiled, driven out, banished, far from our homelands, without any crime we committed, nor any wrong we perpetrated, nor any breach we made in Islam, nor any indecency we did. Al-Nashi' al-Saghir said, from a poem of his of some ten verses, of which al-Hamawi cited his saying:

I marvel at you: you perish, slain by your own sword,
while those who once submitted to you now assault you.
