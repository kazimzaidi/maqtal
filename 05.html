Then Hussein (peace be upon him) called for an inkwell and a sheet of paper, and wrote this testament to his brother Muhammad:
    ثم دعا الحسين ( عليه السلام ) بدواة وبياض وكتب هذه الوصية لأخيه محمد :
In the name of God, the Most Merciful, the Most Compassionate. This is what Hussein ibn Ali ibn Abi Talib bequeaths to his brother Muhammad, known as Ibn al-Hanafiyya: that Hussein bears witness that there is no god but God alone, with no partner, and that Muhammad is His servant and Messenger, who came with the truth from the True One; and that Paradise and the Fire are real; and that the Hour is coming, with no doubt in it; and that God will raise up those who are in the graves. I did not set out in arrogance, nor in insolence, nor as a corrupter, nor as an oppressor. Rather, I set out seeking to reform the nation of my grandfather (peace be upon him and his family). I wish to enjoin what is good and forbid what is wrong, and to follow the way of my grandfather and my father Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him). So whoever accepts me by accepting the truth—then God is most worthy of the truth; and whoever rejects this from me, I shall be patient until God judges between me and the people with the truth, and He is the best of judges. This is my testament to you, my brother, and my success comes only from God; upon Him I rely and to Him I turn.

Then troops of the Muslim jinn came to him and said: O our master, we are your followers and your supporters. Command us with your order and whatever you wish. Were you to command us to kill every enemy of yours while you remain in your place, we would spare you that. So Hussein rewarded them well and said to them: Have you not read the Book of God revealed to my grandfather, the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family): "Wherever you may be, death will overtake you, even if you were in lofty towers," and the Almighty said: "Those for whom killing was decreed would have gone forth to their resting places"? And if I were to remain in my place, by what would these wretched creatures be tested? And by what would they be tried? And who would dwell in my grave at Karbala—which God chose on the day He spread out the earth and made it a stronghold for our followers, and a place of safety for them in this world and the next? But you shall be present on Saturday, the day of Ashura, at the end of which I shall be killed, and after me none of my family, my lineage, my brothers, or my household will remain unsought, and my head will be carried to Yazid—may God curse him.

Then he (peace be upon him) pointed toward Karbala, and the earth sank low until he showed her his resting place, his burial spot, the site of his encampment, his standing place, and his place of martyrdom. At that, Umm Salama wept bitterly and surrendered his affair to God. He said to her: O mother, God Almighty has willed to see me killed and slaughtered, wronged and assaulted; and He has willed to see my women, my kin, and my womenfolk driven away, and my children slaughtered and wronged, taken captive and bound, crying for help yet finding neither helper nor aid.
