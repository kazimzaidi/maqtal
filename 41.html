From Umar ibn Bashir, who said: I asked Abu Ishaq: When were the people humiliated? He said: When Hussein (peace be upon him) was killed, when Ziyad was falsely claimed (as a son of Abu Sufyan), and when Hujr ibn Adi was killed.(2)
    وعن عمر بن بشير قال : قلت لأبي إسحاق : متى ذلَّ الناس ؟ قال : حين قُتل الحسين ( عليه السلام ) وادُّعي زياد وقُتل حجر بن عدي (2) .
Hujr was among the most devoted companions of the Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him). He was a man of knowledge, forbearance, courage, generosity, and eloquence. The Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him) had foretold him of the killing that would befall him afterward. Al-Mas'udi said in his history Muruj al-Dhahab: In the year fifty-three, Mu'awiya killed Hujr ibn Adi al-Kindi. He was the first to be killed in captivity in Islam. Ziyad sent him from Kufa, along with nine of his companions from the people of Kufa, and four from elsewhere. When they had gone some miles from Kufa, headed for Damascus, his daughter—he had no offspring but her—composed these verses:
    وكان حجر من أبرِّ أصحاب أمير المؤمنين ( عليه السلام )   ، وكان ذا علم وحلم وشجاعة وكرم وفصاحة  ، وقد أخبره أمير المؤمنين ( عليه السلام ) بما يجري عليه بعده من القتل  ، قال المسعودي في تأريخه مروج الذهب : وفي سنة ثلاث وخمسين قتل معاوية حجر بن عدي الكندي  ، وهو أول من قتل صبراً في الإسلام  ، حمله زياد من الكوفة  ، ومعه تسعة نفر من أصحابه من أهل الكوفة  ، وأربعة من غيرها  ، فلمَّا صارا على أميال من الكوفة يراد به دمشق أنشأت ابنته تقول ـ ولا عقب له من غيرها ـ :

Journeying to Mu'awiya ibn Harb,

The deceitful ones rejoiced after Hujr,

and not been slaughtered as a camel is slaughtered.

When he reached the meadow of Adhra, twelve miles from Damascus, the courier had gone ahead to inform Mu'awiya of them, so he sent a one-eyed man. When he came in sight of Hujr and his companions, one of them said: If the omen is true, half of us will be killed and the rest spared. He was asked: How do you know that? He said: Do you not see the man approaching, afflicted in one of his eyes? When he reached them, he said to Hujr: The Commander of the Faithful (meaning Mu'awiya) has ordered me to kill you, O head of misguidance, source of unbelief and tyranny, ally of Abu Turab, and to kill your companions—unless you renounce your unbelief, curse your master, and disavow him. So Hujr and a group of those with him said: To endure the edge of the sword is easier for us than what you summon us to. Then to come before God, His Prophet, and his successor is dearer to us than entering the Fire. Half of those with him answered the call to disavow Ali (peace be upon him). When Hujr was brought forward to be killed, he said: Let me pray two units of prayer. He prolonged his prayer, and was asked: Is it out of fear of death? He said: No, but I have never purified myself for prayer without praying, and I have never prayed a prayer lighter than this one. How could I not be afraid when I see a dug grave, a drawn sword, and a spread shroud? Then he was brought forward and slaughtered, and those of his companions who agreed with his position were joined to him. It is said that they were killed in the year fifty.(1)

Among them also was Kumayl ibn Ziyad, may God's pleasure be upon him, who was among the closest companions of the Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him), and held a high station in asceticism and piety. The Du'a of Kumayl, whose recitation is recommended on the eve of the fifteenth of Sha'ban and on the eves of Friday, is attributed to him; the Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him) taught it to him. Sheikh al-Mufid, may God's mercy be upon him, said: Jarir reported from al-Mughira, who said: When al-Hajjaj took office, he sought Kumayl ibn Ziyad, who fled from him, so he deprived his tribe of their stipends. When Kumayl saw this, he said: I am an old man, my life is nearly spent; it is not fitting that I should deprive them of their stipends. So he went out and gave himself over to al-Hajjaj. When he saw him, he said to him: I had longed to find a way against you. Kumayl said to him: Do not bare your fangs at me, nor descend upon me. By God, nothing remains of my life but the dying remnant of dust, so decree what you will decree, for the appointed meeting is with God, and after the killing comes the reckoning. The Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him) told me that you would be my killer. Al-Hajjaj said to him: Then the proof is against you. Kumayl said to him: That is so, if the judgment is yours. He said: Yes, you were among those who killed Uthman ibn Affan. Strike off his neck! So his neck was struck, may God's pleasure be upon him.(2)

Among them was Qanbar, may God Almighty have mercy on him, the freedman of the Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him). It is reported from Abu al-Hasan Ali ibn Muhammad that Qanbar, the freedman of the Commander of the Faithful, was brought before al-Hajjaj ibn Yusuf, who said to him: What service did you used to perform for Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him)? He said: I used to prepare his ablution water. He said to him: What did he say when he finished his ablution? He said: He would recite this verse: "So when they forgot that by which they had been reminded, We opened to them the gates of all things, until, when they rejoiced in what they were given, We seized them suddenly, and behold, they were in despair. So the last remnant of the people who did wrong was cut off. And praise be to God, Lord of the Worlds." Al-Hajjaj said: Did he interpret it against us? He said: Yes. He said: What will you do when I strike off your topmost part? He said: Then I shall be happy and you shall be wretched. So he ordered him killed, may God Almighty have mercy on him.(1)

Among them was Sa'id ibn Jubayr, may God Almighty have mercy on him, who was among the Shia of the People of the House (peace be upon them). He was killed wronged, a martyr in the path of God Almighty. From the book Rawdat al-Wa'izin by al-Fattal al-Naysaburi, he said: Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him) said: Sa'id ibn Jubayr used to follow Ali ibn al-Husayn (peace be upon him) in prayer, and Ali used to praise him. Al-Hajjaj's killing of him was for no reason but this matter. He was upright. It is mentioned that when he was brought before al-Hajjaj ibn Yusuf, he said: Are you Shaqi ibn Kasir ("Wretched, son of the Broken")? He said: My mother knew me better—she named me Sa'id ibn Jubayr ("Happy, son of the Mender"). He said: What do you say about Abu Bakr and Umar—are they in Paradise or in the Fire? He said: Were I to enter Paradise and look upon its people, I would know who is in it; and were I to enter the Fire and see its people, I would know who is in it. He said: What is your opinion of the caliphs? He said: I am not their guardian. He said: Which of them is dearest to you? He said: The one most pleasing to my Creator. The Twenty-Second Gathering
The Killing of Hamza ibn Abd al-Muttalib (peace be upon him)
In a letter that the Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him) wrote to Mu'awiya: A people were martyred in the path of God among the emigrants, and each had merit, until our martyr was martyred, and it was said: Master of the Martyrs. The Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) distinguished him with seventy takbirs in his funeral prayer over him. Do you not see that a people had their hands cut off in the path of God, and each had merit, until when it was done to one of us as had been done to one of them, it was said: The Flyer in Paradise, the Possessor of the Two Wings. And the Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him) continued his words until he said: From us is the Lion of God, and from you is the lion of the confederates.
