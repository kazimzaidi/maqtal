On the authority of Rabah ibn Muslim, from his father, who said: Ibn Muti' said to Umar ibn Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas: You chose Hamadan and Rayy over killing the son of your uncle? Umar said: These were matters decreed from heaven, and I had given my cousin every excuse before the encounter, but he refused all but what he came to. When Ibn Muti' departed and fled from al-Mukhtar, al-Mukhtar marched with his companions to the house of Umar ibn Sa'd and killed him in his home, and killed his son in the worst manner(2).
    وعن رباح بن مسلم  ، عن أبيه قال : قال ابن مطيع لعمر بن سعد بن أبي وقاص : اخترت همذان والريّ على قتل ابن عمِّك ؟ فقال عمر : كانت أمور قضيت من السماء  ، وقد أعذرت إلى ابن عمي قبل الوقعة فأبى إلاَّ ما أتى  ، فلمَّا خرج ابن مطيع وهرب من المختار سار المختار بأصحابه إلى منزل عمر بن سعد فقتله في داره  ، وقتل ابنه أسوأ قتلة (2) .
And Ibn Asakir related, on the authority of Imran ibn Maytham, who said: I was sitting beside al-Mukhtar on his right, with al-Haytham ibn al-Aswad on his left, and he said: By God, tomorrow I shall surely kill a man whose killing will please the people of heaven and the people of earth. He said: Now al-Mukhtar had granted Umar ibn Sa'd safe conduct on the condition that he not leave Kufa except by his permission.
    وروى ابن عساكر عن عمران بن ميثم قال : كنت جالساً عند المختار عن يمينه  ، والهيثم بن الأسود عن يساره  ، فقال : والله لأقتلن غداً رجلا يرضي قتله أهل السماء وأهل الأرض  ، قال : وقد كان أعطى عمر بن سعد أماناً على أن لا يخرج من الكوفة إلاَّ بإذنه.
He said: A man came to Umar ibn Sa'd and said: al-Mukhtar has sworn that he will surely kill a man tomorrow, and by God I do not think he means anyone but you. He said: So he went out until he reached the bath of Umar, and it was said to him: Do you think this is hidden from al-Mukhtar? So he returned and entered his house. When the next day came, I set out and entered upon al-Mukhtar, and al-Haytham ibn al-Aswad came and sat down. He said: Then Hafs ibn Umar came and said to al-Mukhtar: Abu Hafs asks you: will you keep faith with us regarding what was agreed between us and you? He said: Sit down. So he sat down. al-Mukhtar summoned Abu Amra, and a short man came clattering in iron, and al-Mukhtar whispered to him. Then he called two men and said: Go with him. He said: So he went, and by God I do not think he had reached the house of Umar before he came back with his head. Hafs said: Indeed we belong to God, and indeed to Him we shall return. al-Mukhtar said: Strike off his neck. And he said: Umar is for al-Hussein (peace be upon him), and Hafs is for Ali ibn al-Hussein (peace be upon them both)—and they are not equal(3).

And on the authority of Musa ibn Amir, that al-Mukhtar said one day while speaking to those sitting with him: Tomorrow I shall surely kill a man with great feet, sunken eyes, and overhanging eyebrows—a man whose killing will please the believers and the angels brought near.
    وعن موسى بن عامر أن المختار قال ذات يوم وهو يحدِّث جلساءه : لأقتلن غداً رجلا عظيم القدمين  ، غائر العينين  ، مشرف الحاجبين  ، يسرُّ قتله المؤمنين والملائكة المقربين.
He said: al-Haytham ibn al-Aswad al-Nakha'i was with al-Mukhtar when he heard this speech, and it occurred to him that the one he intended was Umar ibn Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas. When he returned to his home, he summoned his son al-Uryan and said: Meet Ibn Sa'd tonight and tell him such and such, and say to him: Take precaution, for he intends no one but you. He said: So he came to him and asked to speak privately, then told him the news. Ibn Sa'd said to him: May God reward your father well for his brotherhood. How can he intend this against me after the covenants and pledges he gave me?
    قال : وكان الهيثم بن الأسود النخعي عند المختار حين سمع هذه المقالة  ، فوقع في نفسه أن الذي يريد عمر بن سعد بن أبي وقاص  ، فلمَّا رجع إلى منزله دعا ابنه العريان فقال : الق ابن سعد الليلة  ، فخبِّره بكذا وكذا  ، وقل له : خذ حذرك  ، فإنه لا يريد غيرك  ، قال : فأتاه فاستخلاه  ، ثمَّ خبَّره الخبر  ، فقال له ابن سعد : جز الله بالإخاء أباك خيراً  ، كيف يريد هذا بي بعد الذي أعطاني من العهود والمواثيق ؟
When al-Mukhtar first appeared, his conduct and his winning of the people were the finest of things. Abdullah ibn Ja'da ibn Hubayra was the most honored of God's creation in al-Mukhtar's eyes because of his kinship with Ali. So Umar ibn Sa'd spoke to Abdullah ibn Ja'da and said to him: I do not feel secure from this man—meaning al-Mukhtar—so obtain safe conduct for me from him. He did so. He said: I myself saw his safe conduct and read it, and it was:
    وكان المختار أول ما ظهر أحسن شيء سيرةً وتألُّفاً للناس  ، وكان عبدالله بن جعدة بن هبيرة أكرم خلق الله على المختار لقرابته بعليّ  ، فكلَّم عمر بن سعد عبدالله بن جعدة وقال له : إني لا آمن هذا الرجل ـ يعني المختار ـ فخذ لي منه أماناً  ، ففعل  ، قال : فأنا رأيت أمانه وقرأته وهو :
In the name of God, the Most Merciful, the Most Compassionate. This is a safe conduct from al-Mukhtar ibn Abi Ubayd for Umar ibn Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas. You are secure by the protection of God for yourself, your family, your wealth, your household, and your children. You shall not be held accountable for any past deed of yours, so long as you listen and obey and keep to your dwelling, your family, and your city. Whoever of God's enforcers and the partisans of the family of Muhammad (may God bless him and his family) and others of the people meets Umar ibn Sa'd shall not confront him except with good. Witnessed by al-Sa'ib ibn Malik, Ahmar ibn Shumayt, Abdullah ibn Shaddad, and Abdullah ibn Kamil. And al-Mukhtar took upon himself the covenant of God and His pledge to keep faith with Umar ibn Sa'd in the safe conduct he had granted him, unless he should commit some deed. And he called God to witness against himself, and God suffices as a witness.
    بسم الله الرحمن الرحيم  ، هذا أمان من المختار بن أبي عبيد لعمر بن سعد بن أبي وقاص  ، إنك آمن بأمان الله على نفسك وأهلك ومالك وأهل بيتك وولدك  ، لا تؤاخذ بحدث كان منك قديماً ما سمعت وأطعت ولزمت رحلك وأهلك ومصرك  ، فمن لقي عمر بن سعد من شرطة الله وشيعة آل محمد ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) وغيرهم من الناس فلا يعرض له إلاَّ بخير  ، شهد السائب بن مالك  ، وأحمر بن شميط  ، وعبدالله بن شدَّاد  ، وعبدالله بن كامل وجعل المختار على نفسه عهد الله وميثاقه ليفين لعمر بن سعد بما أعطاه من الأمان إلاَّ أن يحدث حدثاً  ، وأشهد الله على نفسه وكفى بالله شهيداً.
He said: Abu Ja'far Muhammad ibn Ali (peace be upon them both) used to say: As for al-Mukhtar's safe conduct for Umar ibn Sa'd "unless he should commit some deed," he meant by it: when he entered the privy and relieved himself.
    قال : وكان أبو جعفر محمد بن علي ( عليهما السلام ) يقول : أمَّا أمان المختار لعمر بن سعد إلاَّ أن يحدث حدثاً فإنه كان يريد به إذا دخل الخلاء فأحدث.

He said: al-Mukhtar arose in the morning and sent Abu Amra to him, ordering him to bring him. He came to him and entered upon him and said: Answer the summons. Umar stood and stumbled over a robe of his, and Abu Amra struck him with his sword and killed him, and he brought his head in the lower part of his garment until he placed it before al-Mukhtar. al-Mukhtar said to his son Hafs ibn Umar ibn Sa'd—who was sitting beside him—Do you recognize this head? He said "We belong to God," and said: Yes, and there is no good in life after him. al-Mukhtar said to him: You speak truly, for you shall not live after him. He ordered him killed, and behold, his head was set with the head of his father.
    قال : وأصبح المختار فبعث إليه أبا عمرة  ، وأمره أن يأتيه به  ، فجاءه حتى دخل عليه  ، فقال : أجب  ، فقام عمر فعثر في جبّة له  ، ويضربه أبو عمرة بسيفه  ، فقتله  ، وجاء برأسه في أسفل قبائه حتى وضعه بين يدي المختار  ، فقال المختار لابنه حفص بن عمر بن سعد ـ وهو جالس عنده ـ أتعرف هذا الرأس ؟ فاسترجع  ، وقال : نعم  ، ولا خير في العيش بعده  ، قال له المختار : صدقت فإنك لا تعيش بعده  ، فأمر به فقُتل فإذا رأسه مع رأس أبيه.
Then al-Mukhtar said: This is for al-Hussein, and this is for Ali ibn al-Hussein, may God have mercy on them both—and they are not equal. By God, if I killed three quarters of Quraysh, it would not equal a fingertip of his fingertips. When al-Mukhtar killed Umar ibn Sa'd and his son, he sent their two heads with Musafir ibn Sa'id ibn Numran al-Na'iti and Zibyan ibn Umara al-Tamimi until they came with them to Muhammad ibn al-Hanafiyya, and he wrote a letter to Ibn al-Hanafiyya concerning that(1).

God took vengeance upon him at the hands of al-Mukhtar al-Thaqafi. I said: Glory be to God! He said to me: O Minhal, glorification is indeed good, but at what do you glorify God? I said: O Commander, on this journey of mine, returning from Mecca, I entered upon Ali ibn al-Hussein (peace be upon them both), and he said to me: O Minhal, what did Harmala ibn Kahila al-Asadi do? I said: I left him alive in Kufa. He raised both his hands and said: O God, make him taste the heat of iron; O God, make him taste the heat of iron; O God, make him taste the heat of fire.

So the curse of God be upon Harmala ibn Kahil, who afflicted the People of the House (peace be upon them) by killing the suckling infant Abdullah.
