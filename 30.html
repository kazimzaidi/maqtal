When he was killed I returned to my dwelling, prayed the late evening prayer, and slept. Then someone came to me in my sleep and said: Answer the Messenger of God! I said: What is there between him and me? So he seized me by my collar and dragged me to him, and there was the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family) seated in an open plain, his arms bared, holding a spear, and an angel standing before him with a sword of fire in his hand, slaying my nine companions. Whenever he struck a blow, their souls blazed into fire. I drew near him and knelt before him, and said: Peace be upon you, O Messenger of God. But he did not return my greeting, and remained silent for a long while. Then he raised his head and said: O enemy of God, you violated my sanctity, slew my offspring, did not honor my right, and did this and did that. Sa'id ibn al-Musayyib said: I and the people were diverted from the circling, until the people gathered around him and we crowded upon him. We said: Woe to you! Even were you Iblis, it would not befit you to despair of God's mercy. Who are you? And what is your sin? He wept and said: O people, I know best my own self and my sin and what I have committed. We said to him: Tell us of it. He said: I was a camel-driver for Abu Abd Allah (peace be upon him) when he set out from Medina to Iraq. I used to see that when he wished to perform ablution for prayer, he would place his trousers beside me, and I would see a drawstring that dazzled the eyes with the beauty of its radiance, and I wished it could be mine. Until we reached Karbala, and al-Hussein (peace be upon him) was killed while it was still with him. So I buried myself in a place in the earth, and when night fell, I came out from my place and saw from that battlefield a light, not darkness, a day, not night, and the slain cast upon the face of the earth. Then, in my wickedness and wretchedness, I remembered the drawstring, and said: By God, I shall seek out al-Hussein, hoping the drawstring is in his trousers, that I may take it. I kept looking at the faces of the slain until I came to al-Hussein (peace be upon him), and found him fallen upon his face, a body without a head, his light shining, soaked in his blood, the winds blowing dust over him. I said: This, by God, is al-Hussein. I looked at his trousers as I used to see them, and drew near him, and stretched out my hand toward the drawstring to take it — but he had tied it in many knots. I kept undoing them until I had loosened one knot, then he stretched out his right hand and grasped the drawstring, and I could not pull his hand away from it nor reach it. The accursed soul tempted me to seek something with which to cut off his hands, and I found a piece of a broken sword lying about. I took it, leaned upon his hand, and kept sawing until I severed it from his wrist. Then I moved it away from the drawstring, and stretched out my hand to undo it, but he stretched out his left hand and grasped it, and I could not take it. So I took the piece of sword and kept sawing until I severed it from the drawstring, and stretched out my hand toward the drawstring to take it — when the earth quaked and the sky shook, and there was a great upheaval, weeping and crying out, and a voice saying: O my son! O slain one! O slaughtered one!

When I saw this, I fell unconscious and threw myself among the slain. Then there appeared three men and a woman, with throngs standing around them, and the earth was filled with the forms of people and the wings of angels. One of them was saying: O my son, O Hussein, may your grandfather and your father and your brother and your mother be your ransom. And there was al-Hussein (peace be upon him), seated with his head upon his body, saying: At your service, O my grandfather, O Messenger of God; O my father, O Commander of the Faithful; O my mother, O Fatima al-Zahra; O my brother, slain by poison — upon you all be peace from me. Then he wept and said: O grandfather, they have killed — by God — our men; O grandfather, they have stripped — by God — our women; O grandfather, they have plundered — by God — our caravan; O grandfather, they have slaughtered — by God — our children; O grandfather, it grieves you — by God — to see our condition and what the unbelievers have done to us. And there they were, seated weeping around him over what had befallen him, and Fatima saying: O father, O Messenger of God, do you not see what your community has done to my son? Will you permit me to take from the blood of his grey hair and dye my forelock with it, that I may meet God Almighty stained with the blood of my son al-Hussein? He said to her: Take, and we shall take, O Fatima. And I saw them taking from the blood of his grey hair, and Fatima wiping it on her forelock, while the Prophet and Ali and al-Hasan (peace be upon them) wiped it upon their throats, their chests, and their arms up to the elbows.
