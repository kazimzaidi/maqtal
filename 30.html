She (peace be upon her) undertook this sorrowful journey while her heart was burdened with grief, her spirit was broken, her eyes filled with tears, her body frail and trembling. She had left behind those dearest to her and most beloved, surrounded by widowed women and mourning mothers, and children crying from hunger and thirst. Encircling her were the vile individuals involved in the killing of her family, oppressors of her kin, and ravagers of her caravan, such as Shimr ibn Dhi al-Jawshan, Zajr ibn Qays, Sinan ibn Anas, and Khuli ibn Yazid al-Asbahi, along with Harmalah ibn Kahil, Hajar ibn Abjar, and others of their kind—may God curse them—who lacked any mercy in their hearts. If her eyes filled with tears, whips would descend upon her, and if she wept for her brother, harsh hands would strike her. This was the nature of her journey.

The Second Assembly, on the Eleventh Day: Regarding Those Who Saw the Messenger of God (Peace and Blessings Be Upon Him) After the Martyrdom of Imam Hussein (Peace Be Upon Him)

The narrator said: Abdullah bin Rabah asked the blind judge about his blindness, and he replied: I was present at Karbala but did not fight. I then slept and saw a terrifying figure who said to me, "Go to the Messenger of God!" I said, "I cannot." He dragged me to the Messenger of God (Peace and Blessings Be Upon Him), whom I found sorrowful with a spear in his hand, and before him was a spread mat. A king was standing before him with a sword of fire, striking the necks of people, as fire fell upon them and burned them; then they were revived and killed again likewise. I said, "Peace be upon you, O Messenger of God. By God, I did not strike with a sword, nor did I thrust with a spear, nor shoot an arrow." The Prophet (Peace and Blessings Be Upon Him) said: "Did you not increase the crowd?" Then he let me go and took a bowl with blood in it and applied it to my eyes.

من ذلك الدم  ، فاحترقت عيناي  ، فلمَّا انتبهت كنت أعمى (1) .
Mr. Ibn Tawus, may God have mercy on him, recorded in his book "Al-Malhoof" and Ibn Shahr Ashoub, among others, about Abdullah bin Rabah, the judge. He said: I met a blind man who had witnessed the killing of Hussein (peace be upon him). I asked him about his eyesight, and he replied: I witnessed his killing as one of ten people. However, I neither used a spear, nor struck with a sword, nor shot an arrow. When he was killed, I returned to my home, prayed the evening prayer, and went to sleep. A visitor came to me in my dream and said: Go to the Messenger of God! I said: What do I have to do with him? He grabbed me by my collar and dragged me to him. I found the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family) sitting in a desert, with his arms exposed, holding a spear, and an angel was standing before him with a sword of fire, killing my nine companions. Whenever he struck with the sword, their souls ignited with fire. I approached him, knelt before him, and said: Peace be upon you, O Messenger of God. He did not respond to me and remained silent for a long time. Then, he raised his head and said: O enemy of God, you violated my sanctity, killed my family, and did not respect my rights. You committed this and that. I said: O Messenger of God, I did not strike with a sword, nor did I thrust with a spear, nor shoot an arrow. He said: You speak the truth, but you contributed to their numbers. Come near me! I approached him and saw a bowl filled with blood. He said to me: This is the blood of my son Hussein. Then he smeared my eyes with that blood. I have been awake since then and cannot see anything.

It is narrated from Saeed bin Al-Musayyib that when my lord and master Al-Hussein (peace be upon him) was martyred and the people went to perform the Hajj the following year, I entered upon Ali bin Al-Hussein (peace be upon him) and said to him: "My master, the time for Hajj is near. What do you command me to do?" He replied: "Proceed with your intention and perform the pilgrimage." So, I went for Hajj. While I was performing Tawaf around the Kaaba, I saw a man with amputated hands, his face as dark as a piece of the dark night, clinging to the curtains of the Kaaba. He was saying: "O Allah, Lord of this sacred house, forgive me, but I do not think You will do so."

ولو تشفَّع فيَّ سكَّان سماواتك وأرضك  ، وجميع ما خلقت  ، لعظم جرمي.
Saeed ibn al-Musayyib said: "I was preoccupied and drew people's attention away from the circumambulation until people gathered around me. We said, 'Woe to you, even if you were Satan himself, you should not despair of God's mercy. Who are you, and what is your sin?' He wept and said, 'People, I know myself and my crime well enough.'

We said to him, 'Tell us.' He said, 'I was a camel driver for Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him) when he left Medina for Iraq. I used to see him when he wanted to perform ablution for prayer, he would place his trousers with me, and I would see a belt that dazzled with its radiant beauty. I wished it could be mine. Until we arrived in Karbala and Hussein (peace be upon him) was killed, and I buried myself in a place on the ground.

When night fell, I emerged from my place, and from that battlefield, I saw a light, not darkness, and day, not night, and the slain were strewn over the face of the earth. I recalled my wickedness and misfortune and thought of the belt. I said, 'By God, I will seek out Hussein and hope the belt is in his trousers so I may take it.' I continued to look at the faces of the dead until I came to Hussein (peace be upon him). I found him lying face down, his body without a head, with light shining from him, covered in his blood, and the wind blowing over him. I said, 'This is by God Hussein.' I looked at his trousers as I used to see them and approached him, reaching out to take the belt.

He had tied it with many knots. I kept untying the knots until I untied one from it when he extended his right hand and grabbed the belt. I was unable to remove his hand from it or reach it. My accursed self prompted me to find something to cut his hand off. I found a piece of a sword lying there. I took it and leaned on his hand, cutting and severing it from his arm, then moved his hand away from the belt. I reached out for the belt to untie it, and he extended his left hand, grasping it. I could not take it, so I took the piece of the sword and continued to cut until I severed it from the belt.

When I stretched out my hand for the belt to take it, the earth trembled and the sky shook, with a great commotion and weeping and a voice calling, 'Oh my son, oh my slain, oh my slaughtered one.'"

Oh Hussein, oh stranger! Oh my son, they killed you without knowing you, and they prevented you from drinking water.
واحسيناه  ، واغريباه! يا بنيَّ قتلوك وما عرفوك  ، ومن شرب الماء منعوك.
When I saw that, I was shocked and threw myself amidst the dead. There were three men and a woman surrounded by people standing around them, and the ground was filled with the images of people and the wings of angels. One of them said, "O Son, O Husayn, may your grandfather, your father, your brother, and your mother sacrifice themselves for you." Then, Husayn (peace be upon him) sat with his head on his body, saying, "Here I am at your service, O grandfather, O Messenger of God, and O father, O Commander of the Faithful, and O mother, O Fatimah al-Zahra, and O brother killed by poison, peace be upon you from me." Then he cried and said, "O grandfather, they have killed our men, O grandfather, they have stripped our women, O grandfather, they have pillaged our tents, O grandfather, they have slaughtered our children, O grandfather, it grieves you by God to see our condition and what the disbelievers have done to us." He sat and they wept around him for what had befallen him.

Fatimah said, "O father, O Messenger of God, do you not see what your nation has done to my son? Do you permit me to take some of his gray hair and dye my forelock with it, and meet God Almighty while I am stained with the blood of my son Husayn?" He said to her, "Take it, O Fatimah." I saw them taking some of his gray hair, and Fatimah wiped her forelock with it, and the Prophet, Ali, and Hasan (peace be upon them) wiped their chests, throats, and arms up to the elbows with it. I heard the Messenger of God say, "I sacrifice you, O Husayn! It grieves me by God to see you with your head cut off, covered in dust, your forehead bloodied, your throat bleeding, flung on your back, your torso covered with the sand. You are lying slain, your hands severed, my son! Who cut off your right hand and then the left?"

He said, "O grandfather, there was a camel driver with me from Medina who used to see me when I took off my trousers for ablution. He wished to take them as his prize. The First Assembly, on the Twelfth Day: Their Arrival in Kufa and What Befell Them There

Sayyid Ibn Tawus, may God's mercy be upon him, stated in "Al-Luhuf": "Ibn Sa'd proceeded with the aforementioned captives, and when they approached Kufa, its people gathered to witness them."
