The Thirteenth Gathering
The Imam al-Hussein (peace be upon him) and Yahya ibn Zakariya (peace be upon him)
It is related in the visitation of Jabir ibn Abdullah al-Ansari (may God be pleased with him), on the day he visited al-Hussein (peace be upon him), that he said in his visitation: I bear witness that you are the son of the Seal of the Prophets, the son of the master of the believers, the son of the ally of piety, the descendant of guidance, the fifth of the People of the Cloak, the son of the master of the chiefs, and the son of Fatima, mistress of the women. And why should you not be so, when the hand of the master of the Messengers nourished you, and you were raised in the lap of the God-fearing, and suckled at the breast of faith, and weaned upon Islam? You were good in life and good in death—yet the hearts of the believers are not at peace over your parting, nor do they doubt the goodness of what was chosen for you. So upon you be the peace of God and His pleasure. And I bear witness that you passed away upon the same path as your brother Yahya ibn Zakariya(1).

الإمام الحسين ( عليه السلام ) ويحيى بن زكريا ( عليه السلام )
    جاء في زيارة جابر بن عبدالله الأنصاري ( رضي الله عنه ) يوم زار الحسين ( عليه السلام ) أنه قال في زيارته : فأشهد أنَّك ابن خاتم النبيين  ، وابن سيِّد المؤمنين  ، وابن حليف التقوى  ، وسليل الهدى  ، وخامس أصحاب الكساء  ، وابن سيِّد النقباء  ، وابن فاطمة سيِّدة النسا  ، ومالك لا تكون هكذا وقد غذَّتك كفّ سيِّد المرسلين  ، وربيت في حجر المتقين  ، ورضعت من ثدي الإيمان  ، وفطمت بالإسلام  ، فطبت حياً وطبت ميتاً  ، غير أن قلوب المؤمنين غير طيِّبة لفراقك  ، ولا شاكّة في الخيرة لك  ، فعليك سلام الله ورضوانه  ، وأشهد أنك مضيت على ما مضى عليه أخوك يحيى بن زكريا (1) .
It is related on the authority of Dawud ibn Farqad, who said: The sky turned red when al-Hussein ibn Ali (peace be upon him) was killed, for a year. Then he said: The heavens and the earth wept for al-Hussein ibn Ali and Yahya ibn Zakariya (peace be upon them both), and its redness was its weeping(2).
    روي عن داود بن فرقد  ، قال : احمرَّت السماء حين قُتل الحسين بن علي ( عليه السلام ) سنة  ، ثمَّ قال : بكت السماء والأرض على الحسين بن علي ويحيى ابن زكريا ( عليهما السلام )   ، وحمرتها بكاؤها (2) .
And on the authority of Abd al-Khaliq ibn Abd Rabbih, who said: I heard Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him) say, concerning the words of God, mighty and majestic: "We have not made for him before any namesake"(3): He said: That is Yahya ibn Zakariya—there had been before him none with that name; and likewise al-Hussein (peace be upon him), there had been before him none with that name. And the sky wept only for the two of them, for forty mornings. I said: And what was its weeping? He said: The sun would rise red (and set red)(4).
    وعن عبد الخالق بن عبد ربه قال : سمعت أبا عبدالله ( عليه السلام ) يقول في قول الله عزَّ وجلَّ : « لَمْ نَجْعَلْ لَهُ مِنْ قَبْلُ سَمِيّاً » (3) قال : ذلك يحيى بن زكريا لم يكن من قبل له سمياً  ، وكذلك الحسين ( عليه السلام ) لم يكن له من قبل سمياً  ، ولم تبك السماء إلاَّ عليهما أربعين صباحاً  ، قلت : فما بكاؤها ؟ قال : تطلع الشمس حمراء ( وتغرب حمراء ) (4) .

And on the authority of Jabir, from Abu Ja'far (peace be upon him), who said: The Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) said: Truly, in the Fire there is a place that no one among mankind deserved except by the killing of al-Hussein ibn Ali and Yahya ibn Zakariya (peace be upon them both)(1).
    وعن جابر  ، عن أبي جعفر ( عليه السلام ) قال : قال رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) : إن في النار منزلة لم يكن يستحقّها أحد من الناس إلاَّ بقتل الحسين بن علي ويحيى بن زكريا ( عليهما السلام ) (1) .
And it is related on the authority of Ali ibn al-Hussein (peace be upon him), who said: We set out with al-Hussein (peace be upon him), and he never made camp anywhere nor departed from it without mentioning Yahya ibn Zakariya. And one day he said: Among the signs of the world's worthlessness before God is that the head of Yahya was presented as a gift to a harlot among the harlots of the Children of Israel(2).
    وروي عن علي بن الحسين ( عليه السلام )   ، قال : خرجنا مع الحسين ( عليه السلام )   ، فما نزل منزلا ولا ارتحل عنه إلاَّ وذكر يحيى بن زكريا  ، وقال يوماً : من هوان الدنيا على الله أن رأس يحيى أهدي إلى بغيٍّ من بغايا بني اسرائيل (2) .
And on the authority of Abu Basir, from Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him), who said: Truly, when al-Hussein, the blessings of God be upon him, was killed, the heaven and the earth wept for him and turned red, and they never wept for anyone at all except for Yahya ibn Zakariya and al-Hussein ibn Ali, the blessings of God be upon them.
    وعن أبي بصير  ، عن أبي عبدالله ( عليه السلام ) قال : إن الحسين صلوات الله عليه بكى لقتله السماء والأرض واحمرَّتا  ، ولم تبكيا على أحد قط إلاَّ على يحيى بن زكريا والحسين بن علي صلوات الله عليهم.
And on the authority of Abdullah ibn Hilal, who said: I heard Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him) say: Truly the sky wept for al-Hussein ibn Ali (peace be upon him) and Yahya ibn Zakariya, and it wept for no one besides them. I said: And what was its weeping? He said: They remained for forty days during which the sun rose with redness and set with redness. I said: Is that its weeping? He said: Yes.
    وعن عبدالله بن هلال قال : سمعت أبا عبدالله ( عليه السلام ) يقول : إن السماء بكت على الحسين بن علي ( عليه السلام ) ويحيى بن زكريا  ، ولم تبك على أحد غيرهما  ، قلت : وما بكاؤها ؟ قال : مكثوا أربعين يوماً تطلع الشمس بحمرة  ، وتغرب بحمرة  ، قلت : فذاك بكاؤها ؟ قال : نعم.
And on the authority of Muhammad al-Halabi, from Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him), concerning the words of the Most High: "So the heaven and the earth did not weep for them, nor were they given respite," he said: The sky wept for no one from the time Yahya ibn Zakariya was killed until al-Hussein (peace be upon him) was killed, and then it wept for him.
    وعن محمّد الحلبي  ، عن أبي عبدالله ( عليه السلام ) في قوله تعالى : « فَمَا بَكَتْ عَلَيْهِمْ السَّمَاءُ وَالاَْرْضُ وَمَا كَانُوا مُنظَرِينَ » قال : لم تبك السماء أحداً منذ قتل يحيى بن زكريا حتى قتل الحسين ( عليه السلام )   ، فبكت عليه.
And on the authority of Dawud ibn Farqad, from Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him), who said: The sky turned red when al-Hussein ibn Ali was killed, for a year. Then he said: The heaven and the earth wept for al-Hussein ibn Ali for a year, and for Yahya ibn Zakariya, and its redness was its weeping.
    وعن داود بن فرقد  ، عن أبي عبدالله ( عليه السلام ) قال : احمرَّت السماء حين قتل الحسين بن علي سنة  ، ثمَّ قال : بكت السماء والأرض على الحسين بن علي سنة  ، وعلى يحيى بن زكريا  ، وحمرتها بكاؤها.
And on the authority of Jabir, from Abu Ja'far (peace be upon him), who said: The sky wept for no one after Yahya ibn Zakariya except for al-Hussein ibn Ali, the blessings of God be upon them both, for it wept for him for forty days.
    وعن جابر  ، عن أبي جعفر ( عليه السلام ) قال : ما بكت السماء على أحد بعد يحيى بن زكريا إلاَّ على الحسين بن علي صلوات الله عليهما  ، فإنها بكت عليه أربعين يوماً.

And if the highest moon weeps for his slaughter,
then no moon ever wept save for a moon.
What eyes do not weep blood for you?

The Fourteenth Gathering
God's Vengeance upon the Killers of the Imam al-Hussein (peace be upon him) and Their Followers
at the Hands of the Imam, the Awaited Proof—may God hasten his noble relief
It is related in the Supplication of Lamentation (Du'a al-Nudba): Where is the reviver of the landmarks of the religion and its people? Where is the one who breaks the might of the transgressors? Where is the demolisher of the edifices of polytheism and hypocrisy? Where is the destroyer of the people of corruption and disobedience? Where is the reaper of the branches of error and discord? Where is the eraser of the traces of deviation and base desires? Where is the severer of the cords of lying and slander? Where is the destroyer of the people of obstinacy and rebellion? Where is the one who gives honor to the friends of God and humiliation to the enemies? Where is the gatherer of the word upon piety? Where is the Gate of God through which He is approached? Where is the Face of God toward which the friends of God turn? Where is the connecting link between earth and heaven? Where is the master of the Day of Victory and the unfurler of the banner of guidance? Where is the uniter of the scattered ranks of righteousness and contentment? Where is the one who will seek vengeance for the blood of the prophets and the sons of the prophets? Where is the one who will seek vengeance for the blood of the one slain at Karbala?(2)

O Master of the Age, save us, for we have
no pleasant drink, nor any life of ease.
The nights of waiting have grown long upon us—so will there be,
O son of the Pure One, a morning for the night of waiting?
So anoint with your radiant countenance, for us, eyes
upon whose pupils blindness almost comes.
Here we are, a target for the arrows of calamities—and does
patience avail when the rings of its armor have worn thin?
How long shall the gathering of the oppressors against you be united,
while your gathering is scattered by the hands of your enemies?
So arise—may the remnants of souls be ransom for you—
souls that misfortunes have seized when their endurance failed them.
Suppose your host is small in number—yet your grandfather
met with seventy against an army beyond counting.

It is related from the Imam al-Sadiq (peace be upon him) that he said—in a tradition of his with al-Mufaddal ibn Umar concerning the rising of the Imam, the Proof (peace be upon him): So they shall remain in this state until the Qa'im (peace be upon him) arises. And when the Qa'im arises, they shall meet together at the grave of al-Hussein (peace be upon him), until he comes to Karbala. Then there shall remain no one, of the heavens or of the earth, among the believers, but he shall surround him, visiting him, shaking his hand, and sitting with him upon the throne. O Mufaddal! This, by God, is the elevation above which there is nothing, beneath which there is nothing, and beyond which there is no object of seeking for any seeker(2).
    روي عن الإمام الصادق ( عليه السلام ) أنَّه قال ـ في حديث له مع المفضل بن عمر في خروج الإمام الحجة ( عليه السلام ) ـ : فهم بهذه الحال إلى أن يقوم القائم ( عليه السلام )   ، فإذا قام القائم وافوا فيما بينهم الحسين ( عليه السلام ) حتى يأتي كربلاء  ، فلا يبقى أحد سماويٌّ ولا أرضيٌّ من المؤمنين إلاَّ حفَّ به  ، يزوره ويصافحه ويقعد معه على السرير. يا مفضل! هذه والله الرفعة التي ليس فوقها شيء ولا دونها شيء  ، ولا وراءها لطالب مطلب (2) .
And Sheikh al-Saduq, may God's mercy be upon him, related, on the authority of Abu Hamza Thabit ibn Dinar al-Thumali, who said: I asked Abu Ja'far Muhammad ibn Ali al-Baqir (peace be upon him): O son of the Messenger of God! Why was Ali (peace be upon him) named Commander of the Faithful—a name by which no one before him was named, and which is permitted to no one after him? He said: Because he is the provision-store of knowledge; from him is provision drawn, and he draws provision from no one else. He said: So I said: O son of the Messenger of God! Why was his sword named Dhu al-Fiqar? He (peace be upon him) said: Because he never struck with it anyone of God's creation but that he impoverished him in this world of his family and his children, and impoverished him in the Hereafter of Paradise.

And it is related on the authority of al-Harawi, who said: I said to Abu al-Hasan al-Rida (peace be upon him): O son of the Messenger of God! What do you say concerning a tradition narrated from al-Sadiq (peace be upon him) that he said: When the Qa'im rises, he will kill the offspring of the killers of al-Hussein (peace be upon him) for the deeds of their forefathers? He (peace be upon him) said: It is so. So I said: But the words of God, mighty and majestic: "And no bearer of burdens shall bear the burden of another"(2)—what is their meaning? He said: God speaks truly in all His words. But the offspring of the killers of al-Hussein are pleased with the deeds of their forefathers and boast of them, and whoever is pleased with a thing is as one who did it. And if a man were killed in the East and a man in the West were pleased with his killing, then the one pleased would, in the sight of God, mighty and majestic, be a partner of the killer. And the Qa'im (peace be upon him) will kill them when he rises only for their being pleased with the deeds of their forefathers. He said: I said to him: With what will the Qa'im among you begin when he rises? He said: He will begin with the Banu Shayba and cut off their hands, for they are the thieves of the House of God, mighty and majestic(3).
