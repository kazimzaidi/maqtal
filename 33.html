He (peace be upon him and his family) said: As for my deliberateness in placing and raising my feet during the escorting of the funeral, it was because of the great throng of angels. As for my pronouncing 'God is greatest' seventy times, it was because seventy ranks of angels prayed over her. As for my lying down in her grave, I had mentioned to her during her life the squeezing of the grave, and she said: O my weakness! So I lay down in her grave because of that, until I had spared her it. As for my shrouding her in my shirt, I had mentioned to her during her life the Resurrection and the gathering of people naked, and she said: O what disgrace! So I shrouded her in it, that she might rise on the Day of Resurrection covered. As for my saying to her: Your son, your son — not Ja'far, nor Aqil — when the two angels descended upon her and asked her about her Lord, she said: God is my Lord; and they said: Who is your prophet? She said: Muhammad is my prophet; and they said: Who is your guardian and your imam? She was ashamed to say 'my son,' so I said to her: Say: Your son, Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him). And know, O you who are loyal — may God keep us and you steadfast in loyalty to them and in renunciation of their enemies — that some of the noble reports state that the Family of the House (peace be upon them) attended some of the funerals of their devoted, sincere, loyal followers. Among them was Shatita, may God be pleased with her. It is narrated that she was a believing woman who loved the Family of the House (peace be upon them), and she lived in Nishapur. When the followers in Nishapur sent funds to Imam Musa ibn Ja'far (peace be upon him), she sent a single dirham and a piece of raw cloth spun by her own hand worth four dirhams. The Imam (peace be upon him) accepted what she had sent, and not the rest of the funds, and said to the bearer: Convey my greetings to Shatita, and give her this purse — it was forty dirhams — then he said: And I have gifted her a piece of my own shroud, of cotton from our village Sayda, the village of Fatima (peace be upon her), spun by my sister Halima, may God be pleased with her. It came in some of the visitations of the Imams (peace be upon them): O my masters, were al-Mustafa to behold you, with the arrows of the nation sunk into your livers, and their spears aimed at your throats, and their swords lapping at your blood — the sons of harlots quenching the burning thirst of debauchery from your piety, and the rage of disbelief from your faith — and you between one slain in the prayer-niche whose head the sword has split, and a martyr upon the bier whose shroud the arrows have pierced, and one slain in the open whose head has been raised upon the spear-shaft, and one fettered in prison whose limbs have been crushed with iron, and one poisoned whose entrails have been cut to pieces by the draughts of poison — then surely we belong to God, and to Him we return, and there is no power and no strength save in God, the Most High, the Most Great.

عليهم والبكاء على الحسين ( عليه السلام )
    جاء وجاء في بعض زيارات الأئمة ( عليهم السلام ) : يا مواليَّ  ، فلو عاينكم المصطفى  ، وسهام الأمة معرقة في أكبادكم  ، ورماحهم مشرعة في نحوركم  ، وسيوفها مولغة في دمائكم  ، يشفي أبناء العواهر غليلَ الفُسقِ من ورعكم  ، وغيظ الكفر من إيمانكم  ، وأنتم بين صريع في المحراب قد فلق السيف هامته  ، وشهيد فوق الجنازة قد شكَّت بالسهام أكفانه  ، وقتيل بالعراء قد رُفع فوق القناة رأسه  ، ومكبَّل في السجن رُضَّت بالحديد أعضاؤه  ، ومسموم قد قُطِّعت بجرع السمّ أمعاؤه  ، فإنّا لله وإنّا إليه راجعون  ، ولا حول ولا قوّة إلاَّ بالله العليِّ العظيم.
And one of the poets said, lamenting al-Hussein (peace be upon him):
    وقال بعض الشعراء يرثي الحسين ( عليه السلام ) :
Indeed the calamity of the Family of Muhammad has shattered my body,
and those calamities and afflictions are tremendous.
And there made my eyes weep, by the Euphrates, slaughter-grounds
for the Family of the chosen Prophet, and bones —
Pure bones in the regions of the Euphrates,
for which there is upon us sanctity and a covenant of protection.
How many a noble Fatimid woman taken captive,
and how many a noble one over whom a sword has risen high.
