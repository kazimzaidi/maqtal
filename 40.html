It is said that over all the waters of Ghatafan, on the night al-Hussein, lord of Fakhkh, was slain, a caller was heard crying out and saying:
    وقيل : سُمع على مياه غطفان كلِّها  ، ليلة قتل الحسين صاحب فخ  ، هاتف يهتف ويقول :
O my people, behold the morning's blackened ruin,
and the slaying of the Prophet's children at Baldah!
Let every old man and beardless youth weep for Hussein
from among the jinn—if mankind weeps no laments for you.(1)
أَلاَ يَا لَقَومي لِلسَّوَادِ المُصَبِّحِ وَمَقْتَلِ أولادِ النبيِّ بِبَلْدَحِ ليبكِ حسيناً كلُّ كهل وأمرد من الجنِّ إِنْ لم يَبْكِكَ الإنسُ نُوَّحِ (1)
Likewise, in those days the one who loved the People of the House (peace be upon them) could not feel safe for himself or his children from the enemies of the People of the House (peace be upon them) and their tyrannical helpers, nor was he safe from the rebuke of those who marched in their ranks. Many who loved the People of the House (peace be upon them) were forced to conceal their love and loyalty so as not to be exposed to harm and injustice. Whoever was known in those days for his loyalty to the People of the House (peace be upon them) could find no escape but to hide, to disguise himself, or to flee to where pursuers could not follow. Al-Kumayt, may God Almighty have mercy on him, said:
    وكذلك أصبح محبُّ آل البيت ( عليهم السلام ) آنذاك لا يأمن على نفسه وولده من أعداء أهل البيت ( عليهم السلام ) وأعوانهم الظلمة  ، ولا يسلم أيضاً من عذل أولئك الذين ساروا في ركابهم  ، فقد اضطّر الكثير من محبّي أهل البيت ( عليهم السلام ) أن يخفي حبَّه وولاءَه حتى لا يتعرَّض للأذى والجور  ، فمن عُرف يومئذ بولائه لأهل البيت ( عليهم السلام ) لا ينجيه إلاَّ التستُّر  ، أو التنكِّر  ، أو الهرب إلى حيث لايتبعه الطلب. قال الكميت رحمه الله تعالى :
Do you not see how, out of love for Muhammad's family,
I go about evening and morning in fear, ever watchful—
as though I were a criminal who had committed some crime,
as though, for their sake, I am shunned like one with the plague, out of dread of disgrace?
For what offense, or for what conduct,
am I rebuked and reproached for praising them?(2)
أَلَمْ تَرَني من حُبِّ آلِ محمَّد أروحُ وأغدوا خائفاً أترقَّبُ كأنّيَ جَانٌ مُحْدِثٌ وكأنّني بهم أُتَّقَى من خَشْيَةِ الْعَارِ أَجْرَبُ على أيِّ جُرْم أم بأَيَّةِ سيرة أُعَنَّفُ في تَقْرِيظِهِمْ وأُؤَنَّبُ (2)
And Abu al-Qasim al-Rassi, ibn Ibrahim ibn Tabataba—Ismail al-Dibaj—said, when he fled from al-Mansur to Sindh:
    وقال أبو القاسم الرسي بن إبراهيم بن طباطبا  ، إسماعيل الديباج  ، عندما هرب من المنصور إلى السند :
The blood that tyranny shed of ours has not quenched his thirst
in every land—he has never fallen short in pursuit.
Nothing will cool the burning in his breast
save that he should see no son of a Prophet's daughter living upon it.(3)

And how excellent are the words of the poet of the People of the House (peace be upon them), Ibn Hammad, may God Almighty have mercy on him, when he says:
    ولله درّ شاعر أهل البيت ( عليهم السلام ) ابن حماد رحمه الله تعالى إذ يقول :
Trust not fate, for fate is full of treachery,
two-tongued in this world, and two-faced.
It wronged the descendants of the Guide and scattered them,
so you see no two of them gathered in one place.
As though fate had sworn an oath to disperse them,
like a spiteful one set on rebuke, or a creditor on his due.
Some lie buried at Tayba, and some of them
at Karbala, and some at al-Ghariyyayn,
and the land of Tus and Samarra—and Baghdad
has held two full moons, set within two graves.
O my masters, for whom shall I mourn in grief, and for whom
shall I weep with both eyelids from my two wounded eyes?
Shall I weep for al-Hasan, poisoned and oppressed,
or for al-Hussein, cast down between the two armies?
I weep for him, his gray hair dyed with his own blood,
his cheek in the dust, his two jugular veins severed.(2)

Ibn Shahr Ashub, may mercy be upon him, related in al-Manaqib, from the Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him), that he said to the people of Kufa: "Indeed, there will rise against you a man of wide gullet and bulging belly, who devours what he finds and seeks what he cannot find. Kill him—though you will never kill him. Behold, he will command you to revile me and to disavow me. As for reviling, then revile me; but as for disavowing me, do not disavow me, for I was born upon the natural faith, and I was foremost in Islam and in the migration."(2)
    روى ابن شهر آشوب عليه الرحمة في المناقب  ، عن أمير المؤمنين ( عليه السلام ) أنه قال لأهل الكوفة : أما إنه سيظهر عليكم رجل رحب البلعوم  ، مندحق البطن  ، يأكل ما يجد  ، ويطلب ما لا يجد  ، فاقتلوه  ، ولن تقتلوه  ، ألا وإنّه سيأمركم بسبيّ والبراءة مني  ، فأمَّا السبُّ فسبّوني  ، وأمَّا البراءة منّي فلا تتبرَّؤوا منّي  ، فإنّي ولدت على الفطرة  ، وسبقت إلى الإسلام والهجرة (2) .
And it is related that Abu Jaafar Muhammad ibn Ali al-Baqir (peace be upon him) said to one of his companions: "O so-and-so! What we have suffered from the injustice of Quraysh against us, and their collusion against us, and what our followers and those who love us have suffered from the people! The Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) passed away having declared that we are the most entitled of people over the people. Yet Quraysh conspired against us until they wrested the authority away from its rightful source, and used our right and our argument against the Ansar. Then Quraysh passed it among themselves, one after another, until it returned to us, whereupon our allegiance was broken and war was waged against us."
    وروي أن أبا جعفر محمد بن علي الباقر ( عليه السلام ) قال لبعض أصحابه : يا فلان! ما لقينا من ظلم قريش إيانا  ، وتظاهرهم علينا  ، وما لقي شيعتنا ومحبّونا من الناس  ، إن رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) قُبض وقد أخبر أنّا أولى الناس بالناس  ، فتمالأت علينا قريش حتى أخرجت الأمر عن معدنه  ، واحتجَّت على الأنصار بحقّنا وحجّتنا  ، ثمَّ تداولتها قريش واحد بعد واحد حتى رجعت إلينا  ، فنُكثت بيعتُنا  ، ونُصبت الحرب لنا.
"The holder of the authority remained in arduous, uphill toil until he was slain. Then his son al-Hasan was given allegiance and was bound by covenant; then he was betrayed and abandoned. The people of Iraq turned upon him until he was stabbed with a dagger in his side, his camp was plundered, and the anklets were stripped from the legs of the mothers of his children. So he made peace with Muawiya, sparing his own blood and the blood of his household—who were few indeed. Then twenty thousand of the people of Iraq pledged allegiance to al-Hussein (peace be upon him), then betrayed him, rose against him while his allegiance was upon their necks, and killed him."
    ولم يزل صاحب الأمر في صعود كؤود حتى قُتل  ، فبويع الحسن ابنه وعُوهد  ، ثمَّ غُدر به  ، وأُسلم  ، ووثب عليه أهل العراق حتى طُعن بخنجر في جنبه  ، ونُهبت عسكره  ، وعُولجت خلاخيل أمهات أولاده  ، فوادع معاوية  ، وحقن دمه ودماء أهل بيته  ، وهم قليل حق قليل  ، ثمَّ بايع الحسين ( عليه السلام ) من أهل العراق عشرون ألفاً  ، ثمَّ غدروا به  ، وخرجوا عليه وبيعته في أعناقهم  ، وقتلوه.

"Then the affliction kept growing harsher and increasing until the time of Ubaydullah ibn Ziyad, the killer of al-Hussein (peace be upon him). Then came al-Hajjaj, who killed them by every kind of killing and seized them upon every suspicion and accusation—until a man would rather be called a heretic or an unbeliever than be called a follower of Ali (peace be upon him). And so it came to pass that a man known for his goodness—perhaps pious and truthful—would relate tremendous, astonishing narrations praising some of the rulers who had passed, things God Almighty never created, that never were and never happened, while he believed them to be true because of the multitude who had related them, men not known for lying or for lack of piety."(1)
    ثمَّ لم يزل البلاء يشتدُّ ويزداد إلى زمان عبيدالله بن زياد قاتل الحسين ( عليه السلام )   ، ثمَّ جاء الحجاج فقتلهم كلَّ قتلة  ، وأخذهم بكل ظنّة وتهمة  ، حتى إن الرجل ليقال له زنديق أو كافر أحبُّ إليه من أن يقال شيعة علي ( عليه السلام )   ، وحتى صار الرجل الذي يُذكر بالخير ـ ولعلّه يكون ورعاً صدوقاً ـ يحدِّث بأحاديث عظيمة عجيبة  ، من تفضيل بعض من قد سلف من الولاة  ، ولم يخلق الله تعالى شيئاً منها  ، ولا كانت ولا وقعت  ، وهو يحسب أنها حقٌّ لكثرة من قد رواها ممن لم يعرف بكذب  ، ولا بقلّة ورع (1) .
Abu al-Hasan Ali ibn Muhammad ibn Abi Sayf al-Madaini related in his book (al-Ahdath), saying: Muawiya wrote a single circular to his governors after the Year of Unity: "Immunity is withdrawn from anyone who relates anything of the merits of Abu Turab and his household." So the preachers rose up in every region and on every pulpit, cursing Ali, disavowing him, and slandering him and his household. The people who suffered the most in those days were the people of Kufa, because of the great number of Ali's followers (peace be upon him) there. So he appointed over them Ziyad ibn Sumayya, joining Basra to his charge, and Ziyad would hunt down the Shia, for he knew them well, having been one of them in the days of Ali (peace be upon him). He killed them under every stone and clod, terrorized them, cut off hands and feet, gouged out eyes, crucified them upon the trunks of palm trees, and expelled and scattered them from Iraq, until no one known among them remained there.
    وروى أبو الحسن علي بن محمد بن أبي سيف المدايني في كتاب ( الأحداث )   ، قال : كتب معاوية نسخة واحدة إلى عُمّاله بعد عام الجماعة : أن برئت الذمّة ممن روى شيئاً من فضل أبي تراب وأهل بيته  ، فقامت الخطباء في كلّ كورة وعلى كل منبر  ، يلعنون علياً  ، ويبرؤون منه  ، ويقعون فيه وفي أهل بيته  ، وكان أشدّ الناس بلاءً حينئذ أهل الكوفة; لكثرة من بها من شيعة علي ( عليه السلام )   ، فاستعمل عليهم زياد ابن سمية  ، وضمَّ إليه البصرة  ، فكان يتتبَّع الشيعة وهو بهم عارف; لأنه كان منهم أيام علي ( عليه السلام )   ، فقتلهم تحت كل حجر ومدر  ، وأخافهم  ، وقطع الأيدي والأرجل  ، وسمل العيون  ، وصلبهم على جذوع النخل  ، وطردهم وشرَّدهم عن العراق  ، فلم يبق بها معروف منهم.

And Muawiya wrote to his governors throughout all the regions: that they should not accept the testimony of any of the followers of Ali and his household (peace be upon them). And he wrote to them: "Seek out those among you who are of the partisans of Uthman and those who love him and are loyal to him and who relate his merits and virtues; draw their assemblies near, bring them close, honor them, and write to me of everything each man of them relates, along with his name, his father's name, and his clan."
    وكتب معاوية إلى عمَّاله في جميع الآفاق : ألا يجيزوا لأحد من شعية علي وأهل بيته ( عليهم السلام ) شهادة  ، وكتب إليهم : أن انظروا مَنْ قبلَكم من شيعة عثمان ومحبّيه وأهل ولايته والذين يروون فضائله ومناقبه  ، فأدنوا مجالسهم  ، وقرِّبوهم  ، وأكرموهم  ، واكتبوا لي بكل ما يروي كل رجل منهم  ، واسمه واسم ابيه وعشيرته.
So they did this, until they multiplied the merits and virtues of Uthman—because of what Muawiya sent them of money, garments, gifts, and land grants, which he poured out upon the Arabs and the clients among them. So this multiplied in every city, and people competed for status and worldly gain. No rejected and lowly person would come to one of Muawiya's officials and relate a merit or virtue of Uthman without having his name recorded, being brought near, and his request granted. They continued in this way for a time.
    ففعلوا ذلك حتى أكثروا في فضائل عثمان ومناقبه; لما كان يبعثه إليهم معاوية من الصلاة والكساء والحباء والقطائع  ، ويفيضه في العرب منهم والموالي  ، فكثُر ذلك في كل مصر  ، وتنافسوا في المنازل والدنيا  ، فليس يجيء أحد مردود من الناس عاملا من عمَّال معاوية فيروي في عثمان فضيلة أو منقبة إلاَّ كتب اسمه  ، وقرَّبه وشفَّعه  ، فلبثوا بذلك حيناً.
Then he wrote to his governors: "The narrations about Uthman have become numerous and have spread in every city, in every direction and region. When this letter of mine reaches you, summon the people to relate the merits of the Companions and the first caliphs, and leave no report related by any of the Muslims about Abu Turab without bringing me one to counter it concerning the Companions. For this is dearer to me, more pleasing to my eye, more crushing to the argument of Abu Turab and his followers, and harder upon them than the merits and virtues of Uthman."

Abu al-Hasan Ali ibn Muhammad al-Katib, known as al-Shabushti, mentioned that when Amr ibn al-Hamq was killed, his head was carried to Muawiya, and it was the first head carried in Islam from one land to another. His wife Amina bint al-Sharid was in Damascus, and when the head of Amr was carried to him, he ordered it cast into her lap, and that what she said be heard. When she saw it, she was horrified, and she fell upon it kissing it, and said: "O my woe for him in the house of disgrace! You kept him from me long, then sent him to me slain. So welcome and greetings—I was never one to hate him, and I shall never forget him. Tell Muawiya: May God leave your children orphaned, and make your family desolate of you, and may He never forgive your sin."(1)
