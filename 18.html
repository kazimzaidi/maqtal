It is related that after Rabab returned to Medina, the nobles of Quraysh sought to marry her, but she said: "By God, I shall have no in-law after the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family)." She lived one year after Hussein (peace be upon him), then died of grief over Hussein (peace be upon him), having never sheltered beneath any roof after him.(2)
    وروي أن الرباب بعد رجوعها إلى المدينة خطبها الأشراف من قريش فقالت : والله لا كان لي حمو بعد رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) وعاشت بعد الحسين ( عليه السلام ) سنة  ، ثم ماتت كمداً على الحسين ( عليه السلام ) ولم تستظلَّ بعده بسقف (2) .
Al-Qarmani said: When she returned to Medina, she remained there, knowing no rest by night or day from weeping over Hussein (peace be upon him), and she never sheltered beneath a roof until she died of grief after his slaying, in the year sixty-two of the Hijra.(3)
    وقال القرماني : ولمَّا رجعت إلى المدينة أقامت فيها لا تهدأ ليلا ولا نهاراً من البكاء على الحسين ( عليه السلام )   ، ولم تستظلَّ تحت سقف حتى ماتت بعد قتله كمداً سنة اثنين وستين للهجرة (3) .
The people of Medina wept for Imam Hussein (peace be upon him) and lamented him for three years. It is related from Imam Ja'far ibn Muhammad al-Sadiq, from his father (peace be upon them), who said: Hussein ibn Ali was lamented for three years, and on the very day he was killed, Wa'ila ibn al-Asma', Marwan ibn al-Hakam, Miswar ibn Makhrama, and that company of elders among the Companions of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) would come veiled to listen to the lamentation of the jinn, and they would weep.(4)
    وبكى أهل المدينة على الإمام الحسين ( عليه السلام ) وندبوه ثلاث سنوات  ، روي عن الإمام جعفر بن محمد الصادق  ، عن أبيه ( عليهما السلام ) قال : نيح الحسين بن علي ثلاث سنين  ، وفي اليوم الذي قتل فيه  ، فكان وائلة بن الأصمع ومروان بن الحكم ومسور بن مخرمة  ، وتلك المشيخة من أصحاب رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) يجيئون متقنّعين فيستمعون نوح الجن ويبكون (4) .
Ibn Hamdun said in al-Tadhkira: When Hussein ibn Ali (peace be upon them) was killed, he was lamented in Medina in every house for a full year. Then in the second year he was lamented every Friday, and in the third year every month. Marwan ibn al-Hakam and Miswar ibn Makhrama would enter to them veiled and weep most bitterly until the lamentation came to its end.(5)
    وقال ابن حمدون في التذكرة : لما قُتل الحسين بن علي ( عليهما السلام ) كان النوح عليه بالمدينة في كل بيت سنة كاملة ثم نيح عليه في السنة الثانية في كل جمعة  ، ثم نيح عليه في الثالثة في كل شهر  ، وكان مروان بن الحكم والمسور بن مخرمة يدخلان إليهم مقنعين  ، فيبكيان أشدَّ بكاء حتى ينقضي النوح (5) .

As for the weeping and lamentation of Umm al-Banin (peace be upon her), she wept until even the enemy was moved to pity and wept at her weeping. May God grant her a goodly recompense for the calamity that befell her in her four sons, whom she lost in a single hour.
    وأما بكاء أم البنين ( عليها السلام ) ونياحتها فقد بكت حتى رقَّ لها العدوُّ وبكى لبكائها  ، فأحسن الله لها العزاء بمصابها في أولادها الأربعة الذين فقدتهم في ساعة واحدة.

Abu al-Faraj al-Isfahani said: Umm al-Banin—the mother of these four slain brothers—would go out to al-Baqi' and lament her sons with the most heart-rending and burning lamentation. The people would gather around her to listen, and Marwan would come among those who came for this, and he would not cease to listen to her lamentation and weep. This was reported by Ali ibn Muhammad ibn Hamza, from al-Nawfali, from Hammad ibn Isa al-Juhani, from Mu'awiya ibn Ammar, from Ja'far ibn Muhammad (peace be upon him).(1)
    قال أبو الفرج الإصفهاني : وكانت أم البنين ـ أم هؤلاء الأربعة الإخوة القتلى ـ تخرج إلى البقيع  ، فتندب بنيها أشجى ندبة وأحرقها  ، فيجتمع الناس إليها يسمعون منها  ، فكان مروان يجيء فيمن يجيء لذلك  ، فلا يزال يسمع ندبتها ويبكي. ذكر ذلك علي بن محمد بن حمزة  ، عن النوفلي  ، عن حماد بن عيسى الجهني  ، عن معاوية بن عمار  ، عن جعفر بن محمد ( عليه السلام ) (1) .
Abu al-Hasan al-Akhfash said in his commentary on al-Kamil: She used to go out to al-Baqi' every day to lament him, carrying his son Abdullah, and the people of Medina would gather to hear her elegy—among them Marwan ibn al-Hakam—and they would weep at the anguish of the lamentation.(2)
    وقال أبو الحسن الأخفش في شرح الكامل : وقد كانت تخرج إلى البقيع كل يوم ترثيه  ، وتحمل ولده عبدالله  ، فيجتمع لسماع رثائها أهل المدينة  ، وفيهم مروان ابن الحكم  ، فيبكون لشجيِّ الندبة (2) .
Among the things related concerning the rank of Abu al-Fadl al-Abbas (peace be upon him) and his standing with the Ahl al-Bayt (peace be upon them) is what the learned al-Darbandi, may God have mercy upon him, mentioned in Asrar al-Shahada, citing some of the books of the maqatil. He said: On the Day of Resurrection, when the matter presses hard upon the people, the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) will send the Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him) to Fatima (peace be upon her) to attend the station of intercession. The Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon him) will say: "O Fatima, what means of intercession have you, and what have you stored up for this Day in which is the Greatest Terror?" Fatima (peace be upon her) will say: "O Commander of the Faithful, for this station the two severed hands of my son Abbas suffice us."(3)
    ومما جاء في مقام أبي الفضل العباس ( عليه السلام ) ومنزلته عند أهل البيت ( عليهم السلام ) هو ما ذكره العلامة الدربندي عليه الرحمة في أسرار الشهادة نقلاً عن بعض كتب المقاتل قال : إنه إذا كان يوم القيامة واشتد الأمر على الناس بعث رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) أمير المؤمنين ( عليه السلام ) إلى فاطمة ( عليها السلام ) لتحضر مقام الشفاعة فيقول أمير المؤمنين ( عليه السلام ) : يا فاطمة ما عندك من أسباب الشفاعة  ، وما ادخرت لأجل هذا اليوم الذي فيه الفزع الأكبر فتقول فاطمة ( عليها السلام ) : يا أمير المؤمنين كفانا لأجل هذا المقام اليدان المقطوعتان من ابني العباس (3) .

Also in Asrar al-Shahada, the learned al-Darbandi, may God have mercy upon him, said: A number of trustworthy persons of this present age informed me that one of the believers of this era—who is alive even now—used to visit Hussein (peace be upon him) three times every day, but would only visit Abbas once a week. He saw in a dream the Pure Veracious Lady (peace be upon her) and greeted her, but she turned away from him. He said: "May my father and mother be your ransom—for what failing do you turn away from me?" She said: "For your neglect of visiting my son." He said: "But I visit your son every day." She said: "You visit my son Hussein (peace be upon him), but you visit my son Abbas only seldom."(1)
    وفي أسرار الشهادة أيضاً قال العلامة الدربندي عليه الرحمة : أخبرني جمع من الثقاة في هذا الزمان إن واحدا من مؤمني هذا العصر وهو الآن موجود كان يزور الحسين ( عليه السلام ) في كل يوم ثلاث مرات وما كان يزور العباس إلا في الأسبوع مرة وقد رأى في المنام الصديقة الطاهرة ( عليها السلام ) وسلم عليها فأعرضت عنه فقال : بأبي أنت وأمي لأي تقصير تعرضين عني قالت : لإعراضك من زيارتك ابني  ، قال : أنا أزور ابنك في كل يوم قالت : تزور ابني الحسين ( عليه السلام ) ولا تزور ابني العباس إلا قليلا (1) .
How excellent are the words of one of the poets, when he speaks on the tongue of Hussein (peace be upon him) when Abbas (peace be upon him) was struck down:
    ولله در بعض الشعراء إذ يقول على لسان الحسين ( عليه السلام ) لما صرع العباس ( عليه السلام ) :
O Abu al-Fadl, rise! Were you not the one
who aided me whenever fate assailed?
Today my back is broken by losing you,
my spear is shattered and my hope has failed.
O Banu Hashim, O house of Nizar,
your full moon has fallen—rise in fury!
He turned back toward the tents, his back bent,
cloaked in sorrow from the anguish.
He called: O daughters of Ahmad, be patient—
may God magnify your reward and recompense.
My fate has notched an arrow against me
and struck the hand of my resolve and hit its mark.
Who will protect the helpless ones—he whom, were one to call him
from atop the heights, the terrified would dread?
War grew desolate at losing him by day,
and by night he left the prayer-niche forlorn.(2)

The proof of Islam, Sheikh Hasan Ali al-Badr al-Qatifi, may God have mercy upon him, says, speaking on the tongue of Hussein (peace be upon him):

my ribs—or like the gash of broadswords,
and I went off as the envious wished for me,
when my wing had grown soft to fate.

The Fifth Gathering, of the Seventh Day
Fatima's Plea on the Day of Gathering
and Her Sight of Hussein (peace be upon him)
It is related from Ja'far al-Ahmar, from Abu Ja'far Muhammad ibn Ali al-Baqir (peace be upon him), who said: I heard Jabir ibn Abdullah al-Ansari say: The Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) said: When the Day of Resurrection comes, my daughter Fatima will advance upon a she-camel of the camels of Paradise, its two sides adorned, its halter of moist pearl, its legs of green emerald, its tail of the most fragrant musk, its eyes two red rubies. Upon it is a dome of light whose outside is seen from within and whose inside is seen from without; within it is God's pardon, and without it is God's mercy. Upon her head is a crown of light; the crown has seventy corners, each corner studded with pearls and rubies, glittering as the brilliant star glitters on the horizon of the sky. On her right are seventy thousand angels, and on her left seventy thousand angels, and Gabriel holds the halter of the she-camel, crying out at the top of his voice: "Lower your eyes until Fatima the daughter of Muhammad passes!" On that day there will remain no prophet, no messenger, no veracious one, and no martyr but they lower their eyes until Fatima passes.
