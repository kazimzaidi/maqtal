When Mu'awiya died—and that was in the middle of the month of Rajab in the year sixty of the Hijra—Yazid wrote to al-Walid ibn Utba ibn Abi Sufyan, who was governor over Medina on Mu'awiya's behalf, ordering him to compel Hussein (peace be upon him) to pledge allegiance to him and not to permit him any delay in that. So al-Walid sent for Hussein at night and summoned him. Hussein (peace be upon him) recognized what he intended, so he called a group of his loyal men and ordered them to bear arms, and said to them: al-Walid has summoned me at this hour, and I am not safe from his charging me with some matter to which I will not consent—and he is not to be trusted. So be with me. When I enter upon him, sit at the door, and if you hear my voice raised, enter upon him to ward him off from me.
    فلما مات معاوية ـ وذلك للنصف من شهر رجب سنة ستين من الهجرة ـ كتب يزيد إلى الوليد بن عتبة بن أبي سفيان ـ وكان على المدينة من قبل معاوية ـ أن يأخذ الحسين ( عليه السلام ) بالبيعة له  ، ولا يرخِّص له في التأخير عن ذلك  ، فأنفذ الوليد إلى الحسين في الليل فاستدعاه  ، فعرف الحسين ( عليه السلام ) الذي أراد  ، فدعا جماعة من مواليه وأمرهم بحمل السلاح  ، وقال لهم : إن الوليد قد استدعاني في هذا الوقت  ، ولست آمن أن يكلِّفني فيه أمراً لا أجيبه إليه  ، وهو غير مأمون  ، فكونوا معي  ، فإذا دخلت إليه فاجلسوا على الباب  ، فإن سمعتم صوتي قد علا فادخلوا عليه لتمنعوه عني.
So Hussein (peace be upon him) went to al-Walid ibn Utba and found Marwan ibn al-Hakam with him. al-Walid announced to him the death of Mu'awiya, and Hussein said "To God we belong and to Him we return," then he read out to him Yazid's letter and what he had ordered concerning taking the pledge of allegiance from him. Hussein (peace be upon him) said: I do not think you would be content with my pledge to Yazid in secret, until I pledge to him openly so that the people may know of it. al-Walid said to him: Indeed. Hussein said: Then wait until morning and consider your view in this matter. al-Walid said to him: Depart in the name of God Most High, until you come to us with the body of the people.

Marwan said to him: By God, if Hussein parts from you now without pledging allegiance, you will never again have the power over such a chance—until the slain multiply between you and him. Hold the man and do not let him leave your presence until he pledges allegiance or you strike off his neck. At that, Hussein (peace be upon him) sprang up and said: You, O son of the blue-eyed woman, would you kill me, or would he? You have lied, by God, and sinned. Then he went out walking, with his loyal men, until he reached his house.
