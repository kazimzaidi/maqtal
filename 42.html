And it is reported that the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) said: On the Day of Resurrection, a great multitude of people will be seen at the side of the Path (Sirat), their number known only to God Almighty—they were the lovers of Hamza, and many of them were people of sins and offenses. Then walls will rise between them and their passage along the Path and their crossing into Paradise. So they will say: O Hamza! You see the state we are in. Then Hamza will say to the Messenger of God and to Ali ibn Abi Talib: You see my friends crying out to me for help. Then Muhammad, the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family), will say to Ali, the Ally of God (peace be upon him): O Ali! Help your uncle in rescuing his friends and saving them from the Fire. So Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him) will come to the spear with which Hamza fought the enemies of God in this world, and will hand it to him, saying: O uncle of the Messenger of God, and O uncle of the brother of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family), drive back the Blaze from your friends with this spear of yours, as you used to drive back the enemies of God from the friends of God in this world. So Hamza will take the spear in his hand and place its butt against the walls of fire blocking his friends from crossing into Paradise upon the Path, and will give it one thrust, pushing it back a distance of five hundred years. Then he will say to his friends and the lovers who were devoted to him in this world: Cross. So they will cross the Path safe and secure, the fires having been removed from them and the terrors distanced from them, and they will enter Paradise as winners and triumphant ones.

And from al-Sadiq, from his father, from his grandfather (peace be upon them), he said: Al-Hasan ibn Ali (peace be upon them) said, in his argument against Mu'awiya: Among those who responded to the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) were his uncle Hamza and his cousin Ja'far, and both were slain as martyrs—may God be pleased with them—among a great number who were killed alongside them from the companions of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family). So God Almighty made Hamza the Master of the Martyrs from among them, and granted Ja'far two wings with which he flies with the angels however he wishes from among them—and that was because of their station with the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family), their rank, and their kinship to him (peace be upon him and his family). And the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) prayed over Hamza seventy prayers, from among the martyrs who were martyred with him.

And from Habib ibn Abi Thabit, from Ali ibn al-Husayn (peace be upon them), he said: No one entered Paradise through zeal except for the zeal of Hamza ibn Abd al-Muttalib, and that was when he embraced Islam out of anger for the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family) in the incident of the entrails that were cast upon the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family).

And from Ibn Abbas, he said: The Prophet (peace be upon him and his family) said to me: In a dream, I saw my uncle Hamza ibn Abd al-Muttalib and my brother Ja'far ibn Abi Talib, and before them was a tray of lote-fruit. They ate for a while, then the fruit turned into ripe dates for them, and they ate for a while. So I drew near to them and said: May my father be sacrificed for you both—which deeds did you find most excellent? They said: We were ransomed for you with our fathers and mothers; we found the most excellent of deeds to be sending blessings upon you, giving water to drink, and the love of Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him)(2).

My grief at the slaying of Hamza, and my torment,
will not subside with my sighing and my weeping.
Even until the day of departure, I remain
in my mourning, my sorrow, and my lament.
I shall continue through the days in my anguish for him
until my entrails are torn apart by sorrow.
Far be it that I should forget the calamity of Hamza,
the uncle of the Prophet and Master of the Martyrs—
a hero who, when heroes are counted, has
no equals or peers among them.
By God, the heart of the Chosen One, since his eyes
beheld what the sons of the foundlings had done—
from it the tears poured forth for his uncle,
that great one, the pillar of the mighty.
And Safiyya came to meet her brother,
finding him upon the burning heat of the scorched ground.
She prayed over him and gave him peace, and rose
her sighs, drawing breath with deep gasps.
But Zainab wailed when she beheld
the body of Hussein, his limbs scattered apart,
and the noble lady of Hashim went on calling to him:
Would that, instead of you, my own limbs had been severed!

Ibn al-Jawzi said: When Wahshi, the slayer of Hamza, embraced Islam, the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family) said to him: Keep your face hidden from me, for I do not love to look upon the slayer of the beloved ones. So how, then, would the heart of the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family) bear to see the one who slaughtered Hussein (peace be upon him), ordered his killing, and carried his family upon the bare backs of camels(2)?
    قال ابن الجوزي : ولمَّا أسلم وحشي قاتل حمزة قال له النبي ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) : غيِّب وجهك عنّي  ، فإني لا أحبُّ أن أرى قاتل الأحبة  ، قال هذا والإسلام يجبُّ ما قبله  ، فكيف بقلبه ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) أن يرى من ذبح الحسين ( عليه السلام )   ، وأمر بقتله وحمل أهله على أقتاب الجمال (2) .
And it is reported from Abdullah ibn Mas'ud, who said: We never saw the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) weep more intensely than his weeping over Hamza ibn Abd al-Muttalib when he was slain(3). So how, then, would it have been had he gazed upon his grandson Hussein (peace be upon him) as a mutilated, torn body, scattered apart by the spears? His state, without doubt, would have been more severe than his state on the day of the slaying of Hamza ibn Abd al-Muttalib. As the poet said:

The Twenty-Fourth Gathering
The marriage of Khadija (peace be upon her) to the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family)
and a portion of her virtue and noble life
Sheikh al-Kulayni, may mercy be upon him, narrated from Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him), who said: When the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) wished to marry Khadija bint Khuwaylid, Abu Talib came with the people of his household, and with him a group of Quraysh, until he entered upon Waraqa ibn Nawfal, the uncle of Khadija. Abu Talib began the speech, saying: Praise be to the Lord of this House, who made us of the planting of Abraham and the progeny of Ishmael, settled us in a secure sanctuary, made us rulers over the people, and blessed us in our land in which we dwell. Now, this nephew of mine—meaning the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family)—is one who cannot be weighed against any man of Quraysh but he outweighs him, nor compared with any man but he surpasses him, and he has no equal among creation, even if he is of little wealth. For wealth is a fleeting gift and a vanishing shade. He has an inclination toward Khadija and she has an inclination toward him, and we have come to you to seek her hand for him, with her consent and at her bidding. The dowry is upon me, from my wealth that you have requested, both its immediate and its deferred portion. And he has—by the Lord of this House—a great share, a far-reaching faith, and a perfect judgment.
