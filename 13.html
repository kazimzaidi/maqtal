The Fourth Gathering, of the Fifth Day
What is reported concerning the grave of Imam Hussein (peace be upon him) and the land of Karbala
It is reported from Amr ibn Thabit, from his father, from Abu Ja'far (peace be upon him), who said: God, blessed and exalted, created the land of Karbala twenty-four thousand years before He created the Ka'ba. He sanctified it and blessed it, and it remained, before God created creation, sanctified and blessed, and it will continue to be so until God makes it the most excellent land in Paradise, and the finest dwelling and abode in which God will lodge His chosen ones in Paradise(1).

And from Abu al-Jarud, who said: Ali ibn al-Hussein (peace be upon him) said: God made the land of Karbala a secure, blessed sanctuary twenty-four thousand years before He created the land of the Ka'ba and made it a sanctuary. And when God, blessed and exalted, shakes the earth and sets it in motion, it will be raised up just as it is, with its soil, luminous and pure, and placed in the finest garden among the gardens of Paradise, and the finest dwelling in Paradise—none shall inhabit it but the prophets and the messengers, or he said: those of firm resolve among the messengers. It will shine among the gardens of Paradise as the brilliant star shines among the stars for the people of earth. Its light will overwhelm the eyes of all the people of Paradise, and it will call out: I am the sacred, pure, blessed land of God, that held the master of the martyrs and the master of the youths of the people of Paradise(2).

And from Hammad ibn Ayyub, from Abu Abdullah, from his father, from his forefathers, from the Commander of the Faithful (peace be upon them), who said: The Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) said: My son will be buried in a land called Karbala.     وعن حماد بن أيوب  ، عن أبي عبدالله  ، عن أبيه  ، عن آبائه  ، عن أمير المؤمنين ( عليهم السلام ) قال : قال رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) : يقبر ابني في أرض يقال لها كربلاء  ، هي البقعة التي كانت فيها قبة الإسلام  ، التي نجَّى الله عليها المؤمنين الذين آمنوا مع نوح في الطوفان (2) .
And from al-Fadl ibn Yahya, from his father, from Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him), who said: Visit Karbala, and do not abandon it, for the best of the children of the prophets is held within it. Indeed, the angels visited Karbala a thousand years before my grandfather Hussein (peace be upon him) dwelt there, and not a single night passes without Gabriel and Michael visiting it. So strive, O Yahya, not to be absent from that place.
    وعن الفضل بن يحيى  ، عن أبيه  ، عن أبي عبدالله ( عليه السلام ) قال : زوروا كربلاء  ، ولا تقطعوه  ، فإن خير أولاد الأنبياء ضمنته  ، ألا وإن الملائكة زارت كربلاء ألف عام من قبل أن يسكنه جدي الحسين ( عليه السلام )   ، وما من ليلة تمضي إلاّ وجبرئيل وميكائيل يزورانه  ، فاجتهد ـ يا يحيى ـ أن لا تُفقد من ذلك الموطن.
And from Ishaq ibn Ammar, who said: I heard Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him) say: The site of the grave of Hussein ibn Ali (peace be upon them both) has a known sanctity; whoever recognizes it and seeks refuge in it will be granted refuge. I said: Describe its location to me, may I be your ransom. He said: Measure out from the site of his grave today—measure twenty-five cubits from the direction of his feet, twenty-five cubits from behind him, twenty-five cubits from the direction of his face, and twenty-five cubits from the direction of his head. And the site of his grave, from the day he was buried, is a garden among the gardens of Paradise, and from it there is an ascent by which the deeds of his visitors are carried up to heaven. There is no angel nor prophet in the heavens but that they ask God to grant them permission to visit the grave of Hussein (peace be upon him)—so one group descends while another ascends. And in another narration he (peace be upon him) said: The site of Hussein's grave is a gateway among the gateways of Paradise(3).

And from Abu Hashim al-Ja'fari, who said: I entered upon Abu al-Hasan Ali ibn Muhammad (peace be upon him) while he was feverish and ill, and he said to me: O Abu Hashim, send one of our followers to the Ha'ir(2) to supplicate God for me. So I left him, and Ali ibn Bilal met me, and I informed him of what he had said to me, and asked that he be the man to go. He said: To hear is to obey, but I say: he himself is more excellent than the Ha'ir if he is of the station of those in the Ha'ir, and his supplication for himself is better than my supplication for him at the Ha'ir. So I informed him (God's blessings be upon him) of what he had said, and he said to me: Tell him: The Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) was more excellent than the House and the Stone, yet he would circle the House and touch the Stone. And indeed God, blessed and exalted, has spots that He loves to be supplicated in, so that He answers whoever supplicates Him there—and the Ha'ir is among them(3).

And in another narration he (peace be upon him) said: Why did you not tell him: The Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) used to circle the House and kiss the Stone, yet the sanctity of the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family) and of the believer is greater than the sanctity of the House. And God commanded him to stand at Arafat—these are but places that God loves to be remembered in. So I love that supplication be made for me where God loves supplication to be made, and the Ha'ir is among those places(4).
    وفي رواية قال ( عليه السلام ) : ألا قلت له : إن رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) كان يطوف بالبيت ويقبّل الحجر  ، وحرمة النبي ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) والمؤمن أعظم من حرمة البيت  ، وأمره الله أن يقف بعرفة  ، إنما هي مواطن يحبّ الله أن يُذكر فيها  ، فأنا أحبّ أن يُدعى لي حيث يُحبّ الله أن يُدعى فيها  ، والحير من تلك المواضع (4) .

He said: So I returned to him and told him, and he said to me: It is not so. Indeed God has places that He loves to be worshipped in, and the Ha'ir of Hussein (peace be upon him) is among those places.

المضحكين والساخرين (1) ولله درّ السيد محمد حسين القزويني عليه الرحمة إذ يقول في استنهاض الإمام الحجة المهدي عجل الله تعالى فرجه الشريف :
When, O avenger, will you launch a raid
that returns the foes—and the land turns to a sea of their blood?
Do you turn a blind eye, while you are the seeker of vengeance for a blood,
which, despite guidance, has remained with no avenger?
There, beside al-Tuff, lie the youths of Hashim,
fallen beneath the spear-points, their blood shed unavenged.
No rest until you raise them aloft as quivering lances
of al-Khatt, whose blades do not bend or break,
and send them forth in the raid as neighing
steeds, with victory bound to their manes.
For how many a wound did Umayya inflict upon you,
that until the Gathering no probing shall heal its wounds.(2)
