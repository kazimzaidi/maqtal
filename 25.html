Sheikh al-Mufid (may God have mercy on him) said: And al-Husayn (peace be upon him) called for his mount and rode it, and called out at the top of his voice: O people of Iraq! — and most of them were listening — He said: O people! Hear my words, and do not hasten, until I admonish you concerning what is rightly due to you from me, and until I present to you my excuse. If you grant me what is fair, you will thereby be more fortunate; but if you do not grant me fairness from yourselves, then gather your affair, and let not your decision be obscured to you. Then settle your matter against me and do not give me respite. Indeed, my Protector is God, who sent down the Book, and He is the Guardian of the righteous.
    وقال الشيخ المفيد عليه الرحمة : ودعا الحسين ( عليه السلام ) براحلته فركبها  ، ونادى بأعلى صوته : يا أهل العراق ـ وجلُّهم يسمعون ـ فقال : أيُّها الناس! اسمعوا قولي ولا تعجلوا حتى أعظكم بما يحقُّ لكم عليَّ  ، وحتى أعذر عليكم  ، فإن أعطيتموني النصف كنتم بذلك أسعد وإن لم تعطوني النصف من أنفسكم فاجمعوا رأيكم  ، ثم لا يكن أمركم عليكم غمَّة  ، ثم اقضوا إليَّ ولا تنظرون  ، إن وليّي الله الذي نزَّل الكتاب وهو يتولَّى الصالحين.
Then he praised God and extolled Him, and remembered God with what He is worthy of, and invoked blessings upon the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family), upon His angels, and upon His prophets. No speaker, before him or after him, was ever heard more eloquent than he in speech.
    ثمَّ حمد الله وأثنى عليه  ، وذكر الله بما هو أهله  ، وصلَّى على النبيِّ ( صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم ) وعلى ملائكته وعلى أنبيائه  ، فلم يسمع متكلِّم قط قبله ولا بعده أبلغ منه في منطق.
Then he said: As for what follows — trace my lineage and see who I am. Then return to yourselves and reproach them, and consider: is it lawful for you to kill me and violate my sanctity? Am I not the son of the daughter of your Prophet, the son of his successor and his cousin, and the first believer who affirmed the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) in what he brought from his Lord? Was not Hamza, the chief of martyrs, my uncle? Was not Ja'far the Flyer, who soars in Paradise with two wings, my uncle? Has it not reached you what the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) said concerning me and my brother: These two are the chiefs of the youth of Paradise? If you believe me in what I say — and it is the truth, for by God I have never deliberately told a lie since I knew that God despises those who tell it — and if you disbelieve me, then among you are those who, if you ask them about it, will inform you. Ask Jabir ibn Abdullah al-Ansari, Abu Sa'id al-Khudri, Sahl ibn Sa'd al-Sa'idi, Zayd ibn Arqam, and Anas ibn Malik — they will tell you that they heard this saying from the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) concerning me and my brother. Is there not in this enough to restrain you from shedding my blood?

Then al-Husayn (peace be upon him) said to them: If you are in doubt about this, do you then doubt that I am the son of the daughter of your Prophet? By God, there is between the East and the West no son of a prophet's daughter other than me, neither among you nor among others. Woe to you! Do you seek me for some slain man of yours whom I have killed? Or for some wealth of yours that I have destroyed? Or in retaliation for a wound?
    ثمَّ قال لهم الحسين ( عليه السلام ) : فإن كنتم في شك من هذا أفتشكُّون أني ابن بنت نبيِّكم ؟ فوالله ما بين المشرق والمغرب ابن بنت نبيٍّ غيري فيكم ولا في غيركم  ، ويحكم  ، أتطلبوني بقتيل منكم قتلته ؟ أو مال لكم استهلكته ؟ أو بقصاص من جراحة ؟
But they began to give him no answer, so he called out: O Shabath ibn Rib'i! O Hajjar ibn Abjar! O Qays ibn al-Ash'ath! O Yazid ibn al-Harith! Did you not write to me that the fruits had ripened, and the meadows had turned green, and that I would be coming to an army marshaled for you? Qays ibn al-Ash'ath said to him: We do not know what you are saying. Rather, submit to the judgment of your cousins, for they will show you nothing but what you love. Al-Husayn (peace be upon him) said to them: No, by God, I will not give you my hand like a man humiliated, nor will I submit to you like a slave.
    فأخذوا لا يكلِّمونه  ، فنادى : يا شبث بن ربعي! يا حجّار بن أبجر! يا قيس بن الأشعث! يا يزيد بن الحارث! ألم تكتبوا إليَّ أن قد أينعت الثمار  ، واخضرَّ الجناب  ، وإنّما تقدم على جند لك مجنَّد ؟ فقال له قيس بن الأشعث : ما ندري ما تقول  ، ولكن انزل على حكم بني عمِّك  ، فإنّهم لن يروك إلاَّ ما تحب  ، فقال لهم الحسين ( عليه السلام ) : لا والله لا أعطيكم بيدي إعطاء الذليل  ، ولا أقرُّ لكم إقرار العبيد.
Then he called out: O servants of God! I have taken refuge in my Lord and your Lord lest you stone me, and I seek refuge in my Lord and your Lord from every arrogant one who does not believe in the Day of Reckoning.
    ثمَّ نادى : يا عباد الله! إنّي عذت بربّي وربِّكم أن تُرجمون  ، وأعوذ بربّي وربِّكم من كل متكبِّر لا يؤمن بيوم الحساب.

So al-Husayn (peace be upon him) stood, then said: Perdition be upon you, O assembly, and grief! Was it when, distraught and bewildered, you cried to us for help, and we answered you readily and prepared, that you then drew the sword against us, against our own necks, and kindled against us the fire of sedition which our enemy and yours had stirred up? So you became a faction against your protectors, a hand in support of your enemies against them — without any justice they spread among you, nor any hope that remained for you in them — except for the unlawful gain of this world they granted you, and the base livelihood you coveted, without any offense we committed, nor any wrong opinion that came from us. So why — woe to you! — when you came to hate us and abandon us, did you not, when the sword was not yet drawn, the heart was calm, and the resolve not yet firm — but instead you hastened upon us like the swarming of flies, and rushed upon us like the rushing of moths. So ugliness be upon you! For you are nothing but the tyrants of the nation, the outcasts of the factions, the rejecters of the Book, the spittle of Satan, the band of sin, the distorters of the Book, the extinguishers of the prophetic traditions, the killers of the children of the prophets, the destroyers of the progeny of the successors, those who attach bastards to lineages, the tormentors of believers, and the supporters of the leaders of the mockers — those who made the Quran into fragments.

Yes, by God, treachery is well-known among you; your roots are intertwined with it, your origins and your branches have inherited it, your hearts are firmly set upon it, and your breasts are filled with it. So you became the foulest of things in nature for the hostile, and a morsel for the usurper. Behold, the curse of God be upon those who break their covenants — those who violate their oaths after confirming them, having made God a surety over you. By God, you are indeed they.

And it is narrated that Sa'id ibn Abdullah al-Hanafi stood forth before al-Husayn (peace be upon him) and made himself a target for them as they shot him with arrows. Whenever al-Husayn (peace be upon him) moved to the right or the left, he would stand before him, and he kept being shot at until he fell to the ground, saying: O God, curse them with the curse of Ad and Thamud. Then he died, may God's pleasure be upon him, and there were found upon him thirteen arrows, besides what he bore of sword-strokes and spear-thrusts.

And the Sayyid said: Then Jawn, the freedman of Abu Dharr al-Ghifari, came forward — and he was a black slave. Al-Husayn (peace be upon him) said to him: You have my leave, for you followed us only seeking ease; so do not be afflicted by our path. He said: O son of the Messenger of God! In times of ease I licked clean your bowls, and in times of hardship shall I abandon you? By God, my odor is foul, my lineage is base, and my color is black — so breathe upon me with Paradise, that my odor may become sweet, my lineage be ennobled, and my face turn white.
