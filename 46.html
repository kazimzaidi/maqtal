Among the established historical facts that cannot be denied is the assault of al-Mutawakkil the Abbasid, the Nasibi (2), the denier of the right of the Ahl al-Bayt (peace be upon them), upon the grave of the master of martyrs, Imam al-Hussein (peace be upon him). He first set about forbidding the people from visiting his noble grave, punishing with death anyone who visited it; yet despite this, he found them insistent upon maintaining his visitation and kissing his pure dust. But he found them devoting themselves utterly to coming to his noble grave, even if it cost them their lives. And when he saw that the people would not desist at his prohibition, nor heed it, he set about demolishing the noble grave. He was not content with that, but proceeded to plow it up and lay it waste, uprooting the chest that was around it and burning it, and channeling water over it, so that the place of the bricks caved in and became like a trench. He did all of this in order to obliterate its landmarks for its visitors and lovers, and to erase his memory. But how could that be possible for him? And even if he managed to lay waste the noble grave and demolish it, how could he ever remove al-Hussein's love from the hearts of his Shia and the souls of his lovers?

He appointed over Medina and Mecca Umar ibn al-Faraj al-Rukhaji, who forbade the family of Abu Talib from exposing themselves to ask of the people, and forbade the people from showing kindness to them. Al-Tabari said, concerning the events of the year 236: In it, al-Mutawakkil ordered the demolition of the grave of al-Hussein ibn Ali (peace be upon them), the demolition of the dwellings and houses around it, and that the site of his grave be plowed, sown, and watered, and that the people be prevented from coming to it. It is mentioned that the agent of the chief of police proclaimed in the region: Whoever we find at his grave after three days, we will dispatch him to the dungeon. So the people fled and refrained from going to it, and that place was plowed and what was around it was sown (2).
