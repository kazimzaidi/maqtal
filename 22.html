How excellent are the words of Sheikh Abd al-Husayn al-A'sam, may God have mercy on him, when he says:

My eye weeps for you, not for the sake of reward
but my eye weeps only for your sake.
Karbala is drenched with your blood, yet it is
not drenched with my flowing tears.
Your calamity made us forget the calamities that
went before, and eased the calamities to come.
The tragedies of the days endure a while, then
pass away, while it remains until the Day of Resurrection.
My grief for the riders slain at Karbala —
their appointed times drew near there.
They charged upon the enemies with parched bowels,
while their swords thirsted for the blood of the foes.
They aided the son of the offshoot of their Prophet — blessed are they —
by aiding him they attained lofty stations.

Gabriel and the angels informing

In the Book of Pilgrimage (al-Mazar) by al-Mashhadi, may God have mercy on him, it comes in one of the noble visitations: And I bear witness that you fulfilled God's covenant and His pledge, and all that He stipulated upon you in His Book. You called to His path, and you exhausted your strength in seeking His pleasure. You carried creation along the way of prophethood and the paths of the message, and you traveled in it by the conduct of the prophets and the ways of the successors. Yet no command of yours was obeyed, and no ear listened to you. So may the blessings of God be upon your souls and your bodies (1).

النبي ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) بمقتل الحسين ( عليه السلام )
    جاء في كتاب المزار للمشهدي عليه الرحمة في بعض الزيارات الشريفة : وأشهد أنكم قد وفيتم بعهد الله وذمّته  ، وبكلِّ ما اشترطه عليكم في كتابه  ، ودعوتم إلى سبيله  ، وأنفدتم طاقتكم في مرضاته  ، وحملتم الخلائق على منهاج النبوة ومسالك الرسالة  ، وسرتم فيه بسيرة الأنبياء  ، ومذاهب الأوصياء  ، فلم يُطع لكم أمر  ، ولم تَصغِ إليكم أذنٌ  ، فصلوات الله على أرواحكم وأجسادكم (1) .
Concerning that, one of the poets says:
    وفي ذلك يقول بعض الشعراء :
They pursued you and sought to erase your merit,
but God disappointed whoever coveted that.
How could they, when in the five prayers your remembrance
at the testimony of faith intercedes for the affirmation of Oneness?
تَتَبَّعُوكم وَرَامُوا مَحْوَ فَضْلِكُمُ وخيَّبَ اللهُ مَنْ في ذَلِكُم طَمِعَا أنَّى وفي الصَّلَوَاتِ الخَمْسِ ذِكْرُكُمُ لدى التشهُّدِ للتوحيدِ قَدْ شَفَعا
Abu Basir related from Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him), who said: I heard him say: While al-Husayn (peace be upon him) was with the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family), Gabriel came to him and said: O Muhammad, do you love him? He said: Yes. He said: Indeed your nation will kill him. So the Messenger of God grieved greatly at that. Gabriel said: Would it please you that I show you the soil upon which he will be killed? He said: Yes. So Gabriel folded up what lay between the gathering place of the Messenger of God and Karbala until the two portions met thus — and he joined his two forefingers — and he took up some of the soil with his wings and handed it to the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family). Then the earth was spread out again faster than the blink of an eye, and the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) said: Blessed are you, O soil, and blessed is the one who is killed upon you.

From Anas: that the angel of rain asked permission to come to the Messenger of God, and the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family) said to Umm Salama: Keep the door for us; let no one enter upon us. Then al-Husayn came to enter, but she stopped him, yet he leapt until he got in and began clambering onto the shoulders of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) and sitting upon them. The angel said to him: Do you love him? He said: Yes. He said: Then your nation will kill him, and if you wish I will show you the place where he will be killed. He stretched out his hand, and behold, there was red clay. Umm Salama took it and placed it at the edge of her veil. Thabit said: It reached us that it was the place where he was killed at Karbala.

They did not part until Gabriel descended upon them — or he said: the Lord of the Worlds — and said to them: Your Lord conveys His greetings of peace to you both and says: I have resolved upon you both that you be patient. From Abu Basir, from Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him), who said: Gabriel came to the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) while al-Husayn was playing before the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family), and informed him that his nation would kill him.     وعن أبي بصير  ، عن أبي عبدالله ( عليه السلام ) قال : إن جبرئيل أتى رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) والحسين يلعب بين يدي رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله )   ، فأخبره أن أمته ستقتله  ، قال : فجزع رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله )   ، فقال : ألا أريك التربة التي يقتل فيها ؟ قال : فخسف ما بين مجلس رسول الله إلى المكان الذي قتل فيه حتى التقت القطعتان  ، فأخذ منها  ، ودحيت في أسرع من طرفة العين  ، فخرج وهو يقول : طوبى لك من تربة وطوبى لمن يقتل حولك.
He said: Likewise did the companion of Solomon: he spoke the Greatest Name of God, and the smooth and rugged ground between the throne of Solomon and the throne was folded together until the two portions met, and he drew the throne near. Solomon said: It seemed to me that it came out from beneath my seat. He said: And the earth was spread out again faster than the blink of an eye.
    قال : وكذلك صنع صاحب سليمان  ، تكلَّم باسم الله الأعظم فخسف ما بين سرير سليمان وبين العرش من سهولة الأرض وحزونتها حتى التقت القطعتان فاجترَّ العرش  ، قال سليمان : يخيَّل إليَّ أنه خرج من تحت سريري  ، قال : ودحيت في أسرع من طرفة العين.
From Zayd al-Shahham, from Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him), who said: Gabriel (peace be upon him) announced the death of al-Husayn (peace be upon him) to the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) in the house of Umm Salama. Then al-Husayn entered upon him while Gabriel was with him, and he said: This one — your nation will kill him. The Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) said: Show me the soil upon which his blood will be shed. So Gabriel took a handful of that soil, and behold, it was red soil. And in a narration: it remained with Umm Salama until she died, may God have mercy on her.
    وعن زيد الشحّام  ، عن أبي عبدالله ( عليه السلام ) قال : نعى جبرئيل ( عليه السلام ) الحسين ( عليه السلام ) إلى رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) في بيت أمِّ سلمة  ، فدخل عليه الحسين وجبرئيل عنده  ، فقال : إن هذا تقتله أمتك  ، فقال رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) : أرني من التربة التي يسفك فيها دمه  ، فتناول جبرئيل قبضة من تلك التربة فإذا هي تربة حمراء  ، وفي رواية : فلم تزل عند أمّ سلمة حتى ماتت رحمها الله.

Then he said: Shall I show you some of its soil? He beat his wing and brought out some of the soil of Karbala and showed it to him, then said: This is the soil upon which he will be killed.

Abd al-Rahman said: By God, the cursed Yazid was struck down swiftly and did not enjoy his life after his killing. He was seized suddenly: he slept drunk and woke up dead and discolored, as if smeared with tar. He was taken in regret. And none who followed in his killing or took part in fighting him remained but was afflicted with madness, leprosy, or vitiligo, and that became an inheritance in their offspring — may God curse them (1).
    قال عبد الرحمان : فوالله لقد عوجل الملعون يزيد  ، ولم يتمتَّع بعد قتله  ، ولقد أُخذ مغافصة  ، بات سكراناً وأصبح ميِّتاً متغيِّراً  ، كأنه مطليٌّ بقار  ، أُخذ على أسف  ، وما بقي أحد ممن تابعه على قتله أو كان في محاربته إلاّ أصابه جنون أو جذام أو برص  ، وصار ذلك وراثة في نسلهم لعنهم الله (1) .
From Ibn Abbas, who said: The angel who came to Muhammad (peace be upon him and his family) to inform him of the killing of al-Husayn was Gabriel, the Trusted Spirit, with wings outspread, weeping and crying out, having carried some of his soil, and it gave off fragrance like musk. The Messenger of God said: Will a nation that kills my fledgling prosper? — or he said: the fledgling of my daughter? Gabriel said: God will strike them with discord, so their hearts will be divided (2).
    وعن ابن عباس قال : المَلك الذي جاء إلى محمد ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) يخبره بقتل الحسين كان جبرئيل  ، الروح الأمين  ، منشور الأجنحة  ، باكياً صارخاً  ، قد حمل من تربته  ، وهي تفوح كالمسك  ، فقال رسول الله : وتفلح أمة تقتل فرخي ؟ أو قال : فرخ ابنتي ؟ قال جبرئيل : يضربها الله بالاختلاف فتختلف قلوبهم (2) .
