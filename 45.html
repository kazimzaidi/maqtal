يزيد تمثَّل بهذه الأبيات :
Would that my forefathers at Badr had witnessed

ليت أشياخي ببدر شَهِدُوا جَزَعَ الْخَزْرَجِ من وَقْعِ الأَسَلْ
Mujahid said: he became a hypocrite in this—by God, then by God, there remained no one in his army whom he did not abandon; that is, he disparaged and reproached him(1). And in al-Tabari's narration, he said:
    قال مجاهد : نافق فيها  ، والله ثمَّ والله ما بقي في جيشه أحد إلاّ تركه; أي ذَمَّه وعابه (1) . وفي رواية الطبري أنه قال :
Would that my forefathers at Badr had witnessed
the anguish of the Khazraj beneath the falling spears
We have slain the chief among your masters
and balanced the score of Badr, so now it is even
They raised their voices and cried out in joy,
then said: O Yazid, may your hand never wither
I am not of Khindif if I do not take vengeance
upon the sons of Ahmad for what was done
Hashim toyed with kingship—so neither
news came nor any revelation descended
ليت أشياخي ببدر شَهِدُوا جَزَعَ الْخَزْرَجِ من وَقْعِ الأَسَلْ قد قَتَلْنا القَرْمَ من سَادَاتِكُمْ وَعَدَلْنَا مَيْلَ بدر فَاعْتَدَلْ فأَهَلُّوا واستهلُّوا فَرَحاً ثمَّ قالوا يا يزيدُ لاَ تُشَلْ لَسْتُ من خندفَ إِنْ لم أَنْتَقِمْ من بني أَحْمَدَ مَا كَانَ فَعَلْ لَعِبَتْ هاشمُ بالملكِ فلا خبرٌ جاء ولا وَحْىٌ نَزَلْ
Al-Tabari said: This is the very departure from the faith, the words of one who returns not to God, nor to His religion, nor to His Book, nor to His Messenger (peace be upon him and his family), who believes neither in God nor in what came from God..(2).
    قال الطبري : هذا هو المروق من الدين  ، وقول من لا يرجع إلى الله  ، ولا إلى دينه  ، ولا إلى كتابه  ، ولا إلى رسوله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) ولا يؤمن بالله  ، ولا بما جاء من عند الله.. (2) .

Awana ibn al-Hakam said: When al-Hussein ibn Ali (peace be upon him) was killed, Ubaydullah ibn Ziyad summoned Abd al-Malik ibn Abi al-Harith al-Sulami and sent him to Medina to bring the glad tidings to Amr ibn Sa'id. So al-Sulami entered upon Amr, who said: What news do you bring? He said: What will please the governor—al-Hussein ibn Ali (peace be upon him) has been killed. He said: Announce his killing. So I announced his killing, and by God, I never heard a wailing like the wailing of the women of Banu Hashim in their houses over al-Hussein (peace be upon him). And Amr said, laughing:

The women of Banu Ziyad raised a wailing
like the wailing of our women on the morning of the hare(2)

How excellent are the words of Sayyid Ja'far al-Hilli, may God have mercy upon him, when he says:

By God, what blood was shed at Karbala—
it flowed not upon the earth until it stopped the heavens
What horses of misguidance ran upon the sacred grounds,
trampling the sanctity of God's Messenger, profaned
A day in which the protector of Islam arose,
when the zeal of God's religion was cast aside
He saw that the path of error was being followed,
and people knew not which way guidance had gone
The people returned to their old days of ignorance,
as though he who legislated Islam had spoken lies
A tyrant came to rule over Islam,
spending night and day immersed in vile deeds
I know not where the men of the Muslims had gone,
nor how Yazid came to be a king among them—
The wine-presser, base in his very root,
and of vile nature, who presses out the dripping fat
Though the word of monotheism may run upon his tongue,
his sword has slain by other than monotheism
The faith complained of him of sickness,
yet to none but al-Hussein did it complain
The grandson saw no cure for the upright faith
except when his own blood was shed at Karbala

The Thirty-First Gathering
The Killing of Imam al-Hussein (peace be upon him) and Its Gravity
in the Words of the Companions and Others
Shaykh al-Mufid, may God's mercy be upon him, narrated on the authority of Abdullah ibn Sinan, from Abu Abdullah Ja'far ibn Muhammad (peace be upon them), that he said concerning the death-place of the Master of Martyrs (peace be upon him) and his household: The Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) grieves over their slaughter, and had he been alive in this world that day, he (peace be upon him and his family) would have been the one consoled for them(2).

مقتل الإمام الحسين ( عليه السلام ) وفداحته
في كلمات الصحابة وغيرهم
    روى الشيخ المفيد عليه الرحمة عن عبدالله بن سنان  ، عن أبي عبدالله جعفر بن محمد ( عليهما السلام ) أنه قال في مصرع سيد الشهداء ( عليه السلام ) وأهل بيته : يعزّ على رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) مصرعهم  ، ولو كان في الدنيا يومئذ حيّاً لكان ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) هو المعزَّى بهم (2) .
It is narrated from Muhammad ibn Amr ibn Hasan, who said: We were with al-Hussein (peace be upon him) by the river of Karbala when he looked toward Shimr ibn Dhi al-Jawshan and said: God and His Messenger have spoken the truth. The Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) said: It is as though I see a spotted dog lapping at the blood of my household—and Shimr, may God curse him, was a leper(3).
    وروي عن محمد بن عمرو بن حسن  ، قال : كنّا مع الحسين ( عليه السلام ) بنهري كربلاء  ، فنظر إلى شمر بن ذي الجوشن فقال : صدق الله ورسوله  ، قال رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) : كأنّي أنظر إلى كلب أبقع يلغ في دماء أهل بيتي  ، وكان شمر قبَّحه الله أبرص (3) .
It is narrated from Abu Damra Anas ibn Iyad, who said: Imam Ja'far, God's blessings be upon him, was asked: How long can the fulfillment of a dream be delayed? He (peace be upon him) said: The Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) saw it as though a spotted dog lapped at his blood, and Shimr ibn Dhi al-Jawshan, may God curse him, the slayer of al-Hussein (peace be upon him), was a leper—so the interpretation of the dream came after fifty years(4).
    وروي عن أبي ضمرة أنس بن عياض  ، قال : قيل للإمام جعفر صلوات الله عليه : كم تتأخَّر الرؤيا ؟ فقال ( عليه السلام ) : رأى رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) كأن كلباً أبقع يلغ في دمه  ، فكان شمر بن ذي الجوشن لعنه الله قاتل الحسين ( عليه السلام )   ، وكان أبرص  ، فكان تأويل الرؤيا بعد خمسين سنة (4) .

Thus the blood of al-Hussein (peace be upon him) is from the blood of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family), and he is of him, and the report has been widely transmitted that he said concerning him: Hussein is from me and I am from Hussein. How then could he not be stricken with grief over him? And how could he not be the most sorrowful, anguished, and grieved of all people over him? May God have mercy on Mansur al-Namari when he says, of the grief of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) over his grandson and sweet basil, al-Hussein (peace be upon him):
    فدم الحسين ( عليه السلام ) هو من دم رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله )   ، وهو منه  ، وقد استفاض الخبر أنه قال في حقّه : حسين مني وأنا من حسين  ، فكيف لا يكون مفجوعاً به ؟ وكيف لا يكون أعظمَ الناس حسرةً وتفجُّعاً وحزناً عليه ؟ ورحم الله منصور النمري إذ يقول في حزن رسول الله ( صلى الله عليه وآله ) على سبطه وريحانته الحسين ( عليه السلام ) :
Woe to you, O slayer of al-Hussein, for you
have returned bearing a burden too heavy for the bearer
What gift did you bestow upon Ahmad in
his grave—the heat of a bereaved mother's grief
Come forth tomorrow and seek his intercession,
and rise to come to his pool alongside those who drink(1).
وَيْلَكَ يَا قَاتِلَ الحسينِ لَقَدْ بُؤْتَ بحَمْل يَنُوءُ بالحاملِ أيَّ حِباً حَبَوْتَ أحمدَ في حُفْرَتِهِ من حرارةِ الثَّاكِلِ تَعَالَ فاطْلُبْ غداً شَفَاعَتَهُ وانْهَضْ فَرِدْ حَوْضَه مع الناهلِ (1).
It is beyond all doubt that the first to be consoled over al-Hussein (peace be upon him) is the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family), his grandfather, who would so often offer his own noble self to ransom him, and who would carry him and his brother upon his shoulders. He is his grandson, his offspring, and the fruit of his heart. And al-Hussein (peace be upon him) was the one who recalled the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family), who revived his Sunna and preserved his law.
