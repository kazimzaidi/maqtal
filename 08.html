whose beginning was loyalty and whose end was hypocrisy:
"Come to us—you are a patron and a master;
fate is your slave and time your servant.
Come to us—truly we are your Shi'a,
and you are, above all mankind, our Imam.
Aid us, may God watch over you, for you are our succor,

So he answered them when they called him—and ever
do the noble answer the cry of those who call.
He led for them sturdy steeds, as though, upon
the coursers, they were full moons in perfect completion—
kindlers of war from Luayy ibn Ghalib,
whose resolves no rein could turn aside.
They are the lions—save that they are seas of generosity—
