An arrow-shooter abandoned him amid the piercing of war
with an arrow whose strike slaughtered the throat of the grandson.
So the noble one, brother of generosity, fell from the noble steed—
slain, with the colt neighing around him.
The spearhead of Sinan pierced into his entrails,
and the blade of Shimr had its slashing at his jugular vein.
The storm-winds drag their hems over him,
and from the weaving of the war-horses' hooves he has a tattered garment.
The seven heavens trembled for him, and shaken were
the firm-rooted mountains of the earth, and the sea surged.
O you, the slain one whom the sky wept for in blood—
so the dust of the earth's face is reddened with blood.
His garments in war were crimson with the blood,
yet on the morning of Resurrection they shall be green silk. (1)

They said: He (peace be upon him) stood resting for a moment, having grown too weak to fight. While he was standing there, a stone came and struck him on the forehead. He took the cloth to wipe the blood from his face, when a sharpened, poisoned, three-pronged arrow came and lodged in his chest—and in some narrations, on his heart. Al-Hussein (peace be upon him) said: In the name of God, and by God, and upon the religion of the Messenger of God. He raised his head to the heavens and said: My God! You know that they are killing a man who has no equal upon the face of the earth as the son of a prophet besides him. Then he took the arrow and pulled it out from the back of his neck, and the blood gushed forth like a spout. He placed his hand on the wound, and when it filled, he flung the blood toward the sky—and not a single drop of that blood fell back. And no redness was known in the sky until al-Hussein (peace be upon him) cast his blood toward it. Then he placed his hand a second time, and when it filled, he smeared his head and beard with it, saying: Thus shall I be until I meet my grandfather, the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family), while I am dyed in my own blood, and I will say: O Messenger of God! So-and-so and so-and-so killed me.

Sheikh al-Mufid and Sayyid ibn Tawus, may mercy be upon them, said: They paused for a moment, then returned to him and surrounded him. So Abdullah ibn al-Hasan ibn Ali (peace be upon them)—a boy not yet of age—rushed out from among the women until he stood by the side of al-Hussein (peace be upon him). Zainab, daughter of Ali (peace be upon him), came after him to hold him back, and al-Hussein (peace be upon him) said: Restrain him, my sister! But he refused and resisted fiercely, saying: No, by God, I will not leave my uncle. Abjar ibn Ka'b—and it is said Harmalah ibn Kahil—lunged at al-Hussein (peace be upon him) with his sword. The boy said to him: Woe to you, son of the wicked woman! Would you kill my uncle? So he struck with the sword, and the boy shielded himself with his hand, which it severed to the skin so that it hung dangling. The boy cried out: O mother! Al-Hussein (peace be upon him) took him and clasped him to himself, saying: My nephew! Be patient with what has befallen you, and count it among the good, for God will join you with your righteous forefathers. The Sayyid said: Then Harmalah ibn Kahil shot him with an arrow and slaughtered him while he was in the lap of his uncle, al-Hussein (peace be upon him) (1).

Al-Hussein (peace be upon him) said to him: O son of Dhi al-Jawshan! You are the one calling for fire to burn over my family? May God burn you with fire! And Shabath came and rebuked him, so he felt ashamed and turned away.

قام صلوات الله عليه.
And how excellent are the words of Sayyid Haydar al-Hilli, may mercy be upon him, when he says:
    ولله درّ السيد حيدر الحلي عليه الرحمة إذ يقول :
He met the multitudes alone, yet
in the terror, every limb of his was multitudes.
His spear sprang from his fingers, and it was as if
from his resolve the edge of his sword was forged.
He wedded the sword to the souls, but
their dowry was death, and the bridal dye was gore.
فَتَلَقَّى الجُمُوعَ فَرْداً ولكنْ كُلُّ عُضْو في الرَّوْعِ منه جُمُوعُ رُمْحُهُ مِنْ بَنَانِهِ وَكَأنْ مِنْ عَزْمِهِ حَدُّ سَيْفِهِ مطبوعُ زَوَّجَ السَّيْفَ بالنُّفُوسِ ولكنْ مَهْرُها الموتُ وَالخِضَابُ النَّجِيعُ
And he says in another splendid piece:
    ويقول في رائعة أخرى :
Steadfast, while the earth beneath the warriors
quaked, shaking its mountain Thahlan.
More firm upon the earth than its own back,
when terror unsettled its champions.
The radiance of his face only increased
when fear changed the colors of theirs.
Covered in dust, the warriors, when they beheld him,
felt terror snatch away their colors.
War has never revealed his like—
slain, yet making cowards of its brave men.

He said: Zainab came out of the tent crying: O my brother! O my master! O my family! Would that the sky had collapsed upon the earth, and would that the mountains had crumbled down upon the plain.

He said: And Shimr cried out: What are you waiting for with this man? So they charged him from every side. Zur'ah ibn Shareek struck him on the shoulder, and al-Hussein struck Zur'ah and felled him. Another struck him with a sword on his sacred shoulder, a blow from which he (peace be upon him) pitched forward on his face, for he was already exhausted. He (peace be upon him) kept stumbling and pitching forward, when Sinan ibn Anas al-Nakha'i thrust him in his collarbone, then withdrew the spear and thrust him in the breastbone of his chest. Then Sinan also shot him with an arrow that lodged in his throat. He (peace be upon him) fell and sat upright, pulled the arrow from his throat, and joined his two palms together. Whenever they filled with his blood, he smeared his head and beard with them, saying: Thus shall I be until I meet God dyed in my blood, my right usurped from me. (1)

Humayd ibn Muslim said: Zainab, daughter of Ali (peace be upon him), came out crying: Would that the sky had collapsed upon the earth! O Umar ibn Sa'd! Is Abu Abdullah to be killed while you look on? And Umar's tears were flowing down his cheeks and beard while he turned his face away from her. Al-Hussein (peace be upon him) was sitting, wearing a robe of silk, and the people had drawn back from him. Then Shimr called out: Woe to you! What are you waiting for with him? The wounds and arrows have already overwhelmed him—kill him, may your mothers be bereft of you! So they charged him from every side. The narrator said: He kept pitching down once and rising again. In that state, Sinan charged him and thrust him with the spear and felled him. In one narration, he (peace be upon him) fell upon his right cheek and said to Khawli ibn Yazid: Sever his head! But Khawli weakened and his hand trembled. Sinan said to him: May God shatter your arm and sever your hand! Then Shimr, God curse him, came down to him—and the cursed one was afflicted with leprosy—and kicked him with his foot, throwing him onto his back. Then he seized his beard, and al-Hussein (peace be upon him) said: You are the spotted one I saw in my dream. He said: Do you liken me to dogs? Then he began to strike the throat of al-Hussein (peace be upon him) with his sword.

And it is narrated in al-Manaqib, by his chain of transmission, from Muhammad ibn Amr ibn al-Hasan, who said: We were with al-Hussein at the river of Karbala, and he looked at Shimr ibn Dhi al-Jawshan—who was afflicted with leprosy—and said: God is greatest! God is greatest! God and His Messenger spoke truly. The Messenger of God said: It is as though I see a spotted dog lapping in the blood of my family.
    وروى في المناقب بإسناده عن محمد بن عمرو بن الحسن قال : كنّا مع الحسين بنهر كربلا  ، ونظر إلى شمر بن ذي الجوشن وكان أبرص فقال : الله أكبر ! الله أكبر ! صدق الله ورسوله  ، قال رسول الله : كأنّي أنظر إلى كلب أبقع يلغ في دم أهل بيتي.
And it is said: Shimr and Sinan ibn Anas came to him while al-Hussein (peace be upon him) was at his last breath, his tongue working in his mouth from thirst as he sought water. Shimr—God curse him—kicked him with his foot and said: O son of Abu Turab! Do you not claim that your father is at the Pool of the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family), giving drink to those who love him? Then be patient until you take water from his hand. Then he said to Sinan: Sever his head from the back. Sinan said: By God, I will not do it, lest his grandfather Muhammad (peace be upon him and his family) be my adversary.

Then your announcer stood at the grave of your grandfather the Messenger (peace be upon him and his family) and announced your death to him with pouring tears, saying: O Messenger of God, your grandson and your young man have been killed, your family and your sanctuary have been violated, your offspring taken captive after you, and disaster has befallen your kin and your loved ones. So the Messenger was distressed, and his terrified heart wept, and the angels and the prophets consoled him over you. Your mother al-Zahra was stricken with anguish over you, and the troops of the angels brought near came in succession to console your father, the Commander of the Faithful. And mourning assemblies were held for you in the highest of the high heavens, and the wide-eyed maidens of Paradise struck their faces over you. And the heaven and its inhabitants wept, and the gardens and their keepers, and the hills and their regions, and the earth and its regions, and the seas and their fish, and Mecca and its buildings, and the gardens and their children, and the House and the Station, and the Sacred Place of ritual, and the unconsecrated state and the consecration (1).

And it is narrated that Salma al-Madaniyah said: The Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) handed to Umm Salamah a flask containing sand from al-Taff, and said to her: When this turns into fresh blood, then al-Hussein will be killed. Salma said: A cry rose up from the chamber of Umm Salamah, and I was the first to come to her. I said: What has befallen you, O Mother of the Believers? She said: I saw the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) in a dream with dust upon his head. I said: What ails you? He said: The people leapt upon my son and killed him, and I have just now witnessed him slain. My skin shuddered, and I sprang to the flask and found it boiling with blood. Salma said: And I saw it set before her.

And Zirr ibn Hubaysh narrated, on the authority of Salma, who said: I came in upon Umm Salamah while she was weeping, and I said to her: What makes you weep? She said: I saw the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) in a dream, and upon his head and beard was the trace of dust. I said: What ails you, O Messenger of God, that you are covered in dust? He said: I have just witnessed the killing of al-Hussein (2).

And Sheikh al-Tusi, may mercy be upon him, narrated on the authority of Ibn Abbas, who said: While I was asleep in my dwelling, I heard a great, loud cry from the house of Umm Salamah, the wife of the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family).
