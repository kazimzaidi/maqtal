The Twenty-Seventh Gathering
On the Guardianship of the People of the House (peace be upon them)
Sheikh al-Saduq, may mercy be upon him, narrated, by his chain from Ishaq ibn Rahawayh, who said: When Abu al-Hassan al-Rida (peace be upon him) arrived at Nishapur, and intended to depart from it to al-Ma'mun, the people of hadith gathered around him and said to him: O son of the Messenger of God! You are leaving us without relating to us a hadith that we might benefit from. He had been seated in the covered howdah, so he put out his head and said: I heard my father Musa ibn Ja'far say: I heard my father Ja'far ibn Muhammad say: I heard my father Muhammad ibn Ali say: I heard my father Ali ibn al-Hussein say: I heard my father al-Hussein ibn Ali ibn Abi Talib say: I heard my father, the Commander of the Faithful, Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon them) say: I heard the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) say: I heard Gabriel say: I heard God, majestic be His majesty, say: "There is no god but God" is My fortress; whoever enters My fortress is safe from My punishment. He said: When the mount had passed on, he called out to us: With its conditions—and I am one of its conditions.

The Twenty-Eighth Gathering
What Has Been Reported in Condemnation of Hatred of the People of the House (peace be upon them)
It is narrated from Ibn Sinan, raising it to Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him), who said: Indeed, our discourse is difficult, exceedingly hard; none can bear it but enlightened breasts, or sound hearts, or noble characters. God took a covenant from our followers just as He took it upon the children of Adam, where He, mighty and majestic, says: "And when your Lord took from the children of Adam, from their loins, their descendants, and made them testify concerning themselves, [saying]: Am I not your Lord? They said: Yes"(3). So whoever is faithful to us, God will fulfill for him with Paradise, and whoever hates us and does not render us our right shall be in the Fire, abiding therein forever(4).

And from Abu al-Jarud, from Abu Abdullah (peace be upon him), concerning the words of Him, mighty and majestic: "God has not made for any man two hearts within his breast"(2). He said: Ali ibn Abi Talib (peace be upon him) said: There is no servant of God's servants whose heart has been tested for faith except that he finds our love upon his heart, so he loves us; and there is no servant of God's servants upon whom God is wrathful except that he finds our hatred upon his heart, so he hates us. So we came to rejoice at the love of the one who loves us, and we recognize the hatred of the one who hates us. The one who loves us awaits the mercy of God, mighty and majestic, as though the gates of mercy have been opened for him, while the one who hates us is upon the brink of a crumbling cliff of fire, as though that brink has collapsed with him into the Fire of Hell. So blessed are the people of mercy in their mercy, and woe to the people of the Fire in their abode. God, mighty and majestic, says: "How wretched indeed is the abode of the arrogant"(3). There is no servant of God's servants who falls short in loving us due to some good that God has placed with him, since the one who loves us and the one who hates us are not equal, and the two can never gather in the heart of one man. God has not made for a man two hearts within his breast, loving with one and hating with the other. As for the one who loves us, his love for us is pure, just as gold is purified by fire without any impurity in it; and the one who hates us is at that station. We are the noble ones, and our offspring are the offspring of the prophets, and I am the successor of the successors. The transgressing faction is of the party of Satan, and Satan is among them. So whoever wishes to know our love, let him test his heart; for if he shares in his love of us with our enemy, then he is not of us, and we are not of him, and God is his enemy, and Gabriel and Michael—and God is an enemy to the disbelievers.

The Twenty-Ninth Gathering
The Enmity of the Umayyads Toward Islam and Toward the Family of the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family)
In one of the visitations of the Imams of the People of the House (peace be upon them) it comes: O my masters! Were the Chosen One to behold you, with the nation's arrows sunk deep in your livers, their spears leveled at your throats, their swords plunged into your blood—the sons of harlots slaking the thirst of their iniquity upon your piety, and the rage of disbelief upon your faith—while you were: one struck down in the prayer-niche, his head split by the sword; one martyred upon the bier, his shroud pierced by arrows; one slain in the open, his head raised upon the spear-point; one shackled in prison, his limbs crushed by iron; and one poisoned, his entrails torn apart by the draughts of poison(2). To God we belong, and to Him we return; and there is no might nor power save in God, the Most High, the Most Great.

Al-Qadi al-Nu'man al-Maghribi said: And this pure, deep-rooted enmity, and the seeking of the ancient vengeance of the Days of Ignorance, the accursed Marwan could not conceal—and the joy that came over him at the killing of al-Hussein, may God's blessings be upon him, moved him to take it up in his hand and say what he said.
    قال القاضي النعمان المغربي : وهذه العداوة المحضة الأصيلة  ، وطلب القديم من ثأر الجاهليّة  ، لم يستطع مروان اللعين أن يخفيه  ، وبعثه السرور بقتل الحسين صلوات الله عليه  ، على أن أخذه بيده  ، وقال ما قاله.
And Ali (peace be upon him) had taken him captive on the Day of the Camel, then shown him favor and set him free; yet he observed none of that nor kept it in mind. Rather, Marwan counseled the accursed Mu'awiya to dig up the grave of Ali, may God's blessings be upon him, when he had gained the upper hand in the affair, reciting the verse of the ancient poet:
    وقد كان علي ( عليه السلام ) أسره يوم الجمل  ، فمنَّ عليه وأطلقه  ، فما راعى ذلك ولا حفظه  ، بل قد شاور مروان معاوية اللعين في نبش قبر علي صلوات الله عليه لمَّا غلب على الأمر  ، فتمثَّل بقول الأول :
They buried their brother in the pit and pillowed him—
their brother—yet they cast down Amir without a pillow.
أَجْنَوا أَخَاهم في الحَفِيرِ وَوَسَّدُوا أخاهم وألقوا عامراً لم يُوسدِ
By this he incited him to dig up the grave of Ali (peace be upon him), and reminded him of the slain of Badr from the Banu Abd Shams, and of those of them killed in disbelief, left without pillow nor burial.
    يُحرِّضه بذلك على نبش قبر علي ( عليه السلام )   ، ويُذكِّره قتلى بدر من بني عبد الشمس  ، ومن قتل منهم على الكفر غير موسَّد ولا مدفون.
Then Mu'awiya consulted Abdullah ibn Amir ibn Kurayz concerning the digging up of the grave of Ali (peace be upon him), and he said: I would not wish you to know the place of his grave, nor to ask about it, nor would I wish this punishment to be between us and our people.
    ثمَّ استشار معاوية ـ في نبش قبر علي ( عليه السلام ) ـ عبدالله بن عامر بن كريز  ، فقال : ما أحبُّ أن تعلم مكان قبره  ، ولا أن تسأل عنه  ، ولا أحبُّ أن تكون هذه العقوبة بيننا وبين قومنا.
So Mu'awiya accepted from Abdullah the counsel he gave, and turned away from the opinion of the accursed Marwan in what he had counseled regarding the digging up of the grave of Ali (peace be upon him)—the very one who had granted him favor and freed him from captivity. But the accursed one was overcome by his grudge against the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family); because of those of his kin who were slain in disbelief in God and in associating partners with Him, and his cursing of him, and because Ali (peace be upon him) had brought him to the Messenger of God (peace be upon him and his family) when he sought to banish him, leading him at his command(2).
